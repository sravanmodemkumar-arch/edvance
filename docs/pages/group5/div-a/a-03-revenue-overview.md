## 6. Business Rules

- Revenue targets must be set at the batch level, not just the institute level; a coaching director who only tracks total monthly revenue cannot identify which course type is underperforming — the SSC CGL batch may be exceeding target while the Foundation batch is declining; EduForge's course-wise revenue breakdown allows the Director to intervene with targeted marketing or fee revision before the underperformance compounds
- The peak admission month (June–July for academic year batches) typically contributes 18–25% of annual revenue in a single month; cash flow planning must account for this seasonality — institutes that spend heavily in February–March without reserving for the May–June marketing and operations surge often face liquidity stress; TCC's trend data shows the June 2025 surge of ₹58.6L followed by the May 2025 trough of ₹44.8L — this pattern repeats annually
- Razorpay settlement lag (1–2 days) means the revenue visible in the dashboard today does not reflect cash actually available in the bank; the Director must distinguish between "collected" (payment initiated) and "settled" (funds received in bank account); EduForge shows both figures with an "unsettled" line item; for cash flow decisions (e.g., salary disbursement on the 5th), the Director should use the settled figure, not the collected figure
- GST on coaching services: coaching centres providing competitive exam preparation services (SSC, Banking, RRB) attract 18% GST under SAC code 9992 (other educational support services); only approved schools and colleges are exempt; TCC must collect 18% GST on all fee invoices and remit monthly via GSTR-3B; the ₹48.2L collected represents fee inclusive of GST — the actual revenue to TCC is ₹48.2L / 1.18 = ₹40.8L; failure to remit GST on time attracts 18% per annum interest
- The revenue overview page is visible only to the Director (K7) and Finance Manager (K5); Branch Managers (K6) see only their own branch's collection data; the Director can see all branches but cannot see individual student payment details from this page — that level of detail requires navigating to the Fee Collection page in Division G; this separation prevents Directors from inadvertently exposing student financial information during presentations on screens visible to others
