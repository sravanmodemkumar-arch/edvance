## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/summary/` | Dashboard key metrics (question count, revenue, quality score) |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/revenue/?month=2026-03` | Revenue breakdown for a specific month |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/revenue/trend/?months=6` | Revenue trend over N months |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/content-status/` | Question pipeline (approved, pending, rejected counts) |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/activity/?days=7` | Recent activity feed |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/pending-actions/` | Pending actions requiring partner response |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/quality-score/history/` | Quality score history with factor breakdown |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/team/` | Team members list (publisher accounts) |
| 9 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/revenue/download/?format=csv` | Download revenue report as CSV/PDF |
| 10 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/dashboard/notifications/` | Unread notifications and alerts |

## 5. Business Rules

- The partner dashboard is the single most important retention surface for content partners; Dr. Venkat Rao logs in 4-5 times per week to check his revenue accrual, review feedback on submitted questions, and monitor his quality score; the dashboard must load within 2 seconds even for Disha Publications with 2,40,000 questions, which means all aggregate metrics (total questions, active questions, revenue, quality score) are pre-computed and cached in Redis with a 15-minute TTL, refreshed by a background Celery task; the revenue chart uses denormalised monthly aggregates stored in a `partner_revenue_monthly` table rather than computing from raw usage logs on every page load.

- Revenue visibility follows a strict timeline: the partner can see real-time estimated revenue for the current month (updated every 6 hours based on interim usage data), finalised revenue for the previous month (locked on the 5th of the following month after reconciliation), and payout status (initiated by the 10th, credited by the 15th); Dr. Venkat Rao seeing "Rs. 38,000" for March 2026 on March 31 is an estimate that may change by up to 5% after final reconciliation; the dashboard clearly labels estimates as "Estimated" and finalised amounts as "Final" to prevent disputes; the download revenue report endpoint generates a detailed CSV with per-question, per-TSP, per-exam usage data that the partner can use for their own accounting and tax filing purposes.

- The pending actions section surfaces time-sensitive items that directly impact the partner's content quality and earnings; reviewer feedback on questions must be addressed within 7 days or the questions are auto-rejected, which hurts the partner's acceptance rate and quality score; aspirant error reports (flagged by students who found mistakes in questions) must be acknowledged within 48 hours — Dr. Venkat Rao's 3 flagged questions (Q-ID 84201, 84209, 84215) each represent a real student who encountered a problem during exam preparation, and unresolved errors erode aspirant trust in EduForge's content pool; the dashboard sorts pending actions by urgency (error reports first, then reviewer feedback, then informational items) and sends email reminders at 24 hours and 48 hours before the action deadline.

- Publisher accounts like Disha Publications have a team management layer that individual SMEs do not; Rajesh Sharma (Admin) can invite team members, assign roles (Content Lead, Author, Viewer), and see per-member contribution statistics; each team member has their own login but all content is attributed to the publisher entity for revenue and agreement purposes; the Admin can restrict team members from seeing revenue data (e.g., authors see only their question stats, not the Rs. 4.8L monthly revenue); activity logs capture which team member uploaded or edited each question, enabling internal accountability at Disha Publications without exposing commercially sensitive revenue data to junior content authors who may leave and join a competitor.
