## 5. Business Rules

- Academic credential verification for individual SMEs requires the Content Team to confirm the claimed institution, qualification, and years of experience against the uploaded documents (degree certificate, experience letter, or university ID card); Dr. Venkat Rao's claim of a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Andhra University with 35 years of teaching experience is verified by checking his uploaded degree certificate and cross-referencing with publicly available faculty records; a partner who claims a Ph.D. but uploads a B.Sc. certificate is flagged for discrepancy and the registration is held until clarified; this verification protects EduForge's content quality reputation because aspirants and TSPs trust that questions authored by "Dr. Venkat Rao, Ph.D. Mathematics, AU" are written by a genuine subject matter expert, not an impersonator.

- Bank account verification uses the penny-drop method: EduForge deposits Re. 1 to the submitted bank account via IMPS/NEFT and the partner confirms receipt; for Dr. Venkat Rao, the penny drop goes to his SBI Visakhapatnam account ending in 4521; for Disha Publications, it goes to their HDFC Connaught Place account ending in 8832; the bank account name returned by the payment gateway must match the partner's registered name (for individuals) or organisation name (for companies) within a fuzzy-match threshold of 85%; this prevents revenue payouts to incorrect or fraudulent bank accounts; bank details can only be changed once every 90 days, and a change triggers re-verification and a 7-day cooling period before the next payout is processed to the new account.

- The partner quality score (0.0 to 5.0) is computed as a weighted average of four factors: question acceptance rate (40% weight), average question difficulty accuracy (20% weight, measuring whether the partner's self-assigned difficulty matches reviewer assessment), error rate flagged by aspirants post-publication (20% weight), and content freshness measured by upload frequency and recency (20% weight); Dr. Venkat Rao's score of 4.6 reflects his 98.5% acceptance rate, accurate difficulty tagging, very low aspirant-reported error rate (0.3%), and consistent monthly uploads of 400-500 questions; a score below 3.0 triggers a quality improvement plan where the Content Team provides feedback and the partner must improve within 60 days or face account suspension; scores are recalculated weekly every Monday at 02:00 IST.

- The verification queue operates on a 48-hour SLA from submission to first review; if a registration remains unreviewed for 72 hours, it auto-escalates to the Content Lead with a Slack notification; the Content Team reviewer checks five things in order: (1) PAN/GSTIN validation pass from automated checks, (2) document authenticity (no tampering, readable scans), (3) academic or publishing credentials match claims, (4) sample content quality (minimum score 3.0 out of 5.0 based on 50-question sample review), and (5) no duplicate or blacklisted partner; rejection requires a mandatory reason field that is emailed to the partner along with resubmission instructions; a partner can resubmit up to 3 times; after 3 rejections, the case is escalated to the Content Head for a final decision.
