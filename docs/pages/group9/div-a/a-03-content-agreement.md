## 5. Business Rules

- The revenue share model of Rs. 0.02 per student per question per month is calculated at the end of each calendar month by counting the distinct student-question interactions across all TSP tenants that have licensed the partner's content; "interaction" means the question appeared in a mock test, practice set, or study material accessed by a student — it does not require the student to answer the question, only that the question was rendered on the student's screen; Dr. Venkat Rao's 18,500 questions earn differently based on how many TSPs licence them and how many students those TSPs serve; a question used by 5 TSPs with 10,000 students each counts the same as one TSP with 50,000 students because the metric is per-student, not per-TSP; this ensures partners earn proportionally to the actual reach and usage of their content.

- Exclusivity tiers create a clear trade-off between partner freedom and revenue share; non-exclusive (70/30) is the default and recommended for most partners because it allows Dr. Venkat Rao to also sell his questions to competing platforms like Testbook or Oliveboard, or publish them in his own book; exclusive (80/20) pays a higher share because the partner surrenders competing digital distribution, meaning EduForge's investment in marketing and featuring the content is protected from being undermined by the same content appearing on a competitor at a lower price; IP assignment (50/50 plus lump sum) is designed for content agencies like Telugu Vidya Content who create work-for-hire content and prefer upfront payment over long-term royalties; the agreement type cannot be changed mid-term and only takes effect upon renewal.

- Digital signing via Aadhaar eSign is the primary method because it is legally equivalent to a handwritten signature under the Information Technology Act 2000 (Section 3A) and can be completed entirely online in under 3 minutes; for corporate entities like Disha Publications, DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) Class 2 or above is required because the signatory must demonstrate authorisation to sign on behalf of the company; the system verifies the DSC against the Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA) database to confirm validity and non-revocation; physical signature is offered as a fallback but adds 5-7 business days for courier, verification, and filing; all signed agreements are stored with tamper-evident hashes (SHA-256) and timestamped using a TSA (Time Stamping Authority) to ensure non-repudiation; the signed PDF is emailed to both the partner and EduForge Legal within 1 hour of signing.

- Agreement termination requires a 90-day notice period to protect both parties: the partner's content continues earning revenue during the notice period, and TSPs that have licensed the content through EduForge get 90 days to find alternatives or renew directly; upon termination, EduForge removes the partner's content from all active mock tests and question pools within 30 days after the notice period ends (total 120 days from notice); any revenue earned during the notice period is paid out in the regular monthly cycle; Dr. Venkat Rao cannot retroactively claim revenue for usage before the agreement start date, and EduForge cannot retroactively claw back payments already made; disputes are resolved through arbitration in Hyderabad under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996, with the language of arbitration being English.
