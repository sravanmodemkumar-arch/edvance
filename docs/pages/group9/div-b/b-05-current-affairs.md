> **Priority:** P2
> **Roles:** Content Partner · EduForge Editorial Team (review queue)

---

## Overview

The Current Affairs Submission module enables content partners to contribute time-sensitive news items, event summaries, and news-to-question conversions that keep EduForge's current affairs database fresh for competitive exam aspirants. While Dr. Venkat Rao primarily focuses on quantitative aptitude questions, this module is heavily used by current affairs specialists and editorial agencies who submit daily and weekly entries covering Polity, Economy, Science & Technology, and state-specific news (Andhra Pradesh, Telangana). Each entry is date-tagged, categorized, linked to exam relevance, and optionally converted into practice questions.

## Business Rules

1. Every current affairs entry must include an event date (the date the news event occurred), a publish date (when it should become visible to students), a headline of no more than 200 characters, a summary of 150 to 300 words, at least three key points in bullet format for quick student revision, and a valid source URL that the editorial team can verify. The event date must not be more than 7 days in the past for daily CA entries, because stale current affairs lose their relevance for exam preparation and the editorial team prioritizes entries within a 48-hour window of the event. Weekly roundup entries are permitted to reference events from the preceding 7 days, and monthly digests can span the entire preceding month. The publish date can be set to a future date to allow the editorial team review time, but it cannot be set more than 14 days ahead of the event date because current affairs content that sits unpublished for too long risks becoming outdated by subsequent developments. Each entry must be categorized into exactly one primary category and one sub-category from the predefined taxonomy, and must be tagged with at least one relevant exam so the platform can surface it in exam-specific current affairs feeds.

2. The news-to-question conversion feature uses a large language model fine-tuned on 50,000 previously approved current affairs questions to generate MCQ candidates from the summary and key points of a CA entry. The model typically produces 3 to 5 candidate questions per entry, each with four options, a correct answer, and a brief explanation referencing the source material. All auto-generated questions are clearly flagged as AI-generated and require explicit partner review and approval before they enter the question bank, because the model occasionally produces factually incorrect distractors or ambiguous phrasing that would confuse students. The partner can edit any aspect of a generated question (text, options, explanation, difficulty, exam tags) through the same interface used for manual question authoring, and can also add entirely new questions manually that are linked to the CA entry for provenance tracking. Questions generated from current affairs entries inherit the event date tag, which allows the platform to surface them in date-filtered practice sets (e.g., "March 2026 Current Affairs Quiz") and to automatically retire them from active practice after 12 months when they are no longer considered "current" for exam purposes.

3. The calendar view aggregates current affairs entries by date and provides a visual density map that helps both partners and the editorial team identify coverage gaps. If a date has fewer than 2 entries from any partner in the system, it is highlighted in yellow as a coverage gap, and partners who have opted into gap-fill notifications receive an alert suggesting they contribute entries for that date. The editorial team uses the calendar view to ensure that no major news event goes uncovered in the EduForge current affairs database, comparing the platform's entries against a curated list of significant events from wire services (PTI, Reuters). Category-level filtering on the calendar reveals thematic gaps — for instance, if March 2026 has strong Economy coverage but weak Science and Technology coverage, the editorial team can reach out to specialist partners. The monthly statistics panel shows each partner's contribution volume, approval rate, and student engagement metrics, which factor into the partner's performance score and influence their content visibility ranking in the platform.

4. Current affairs entries follow an expedited review lifecycle compared to regular questions and study materials, with a target turnaround of 24 hours for daily CA entries and 48 hours for weekly roundups, because the value of current affairs content degrades rapidly with time. When a partner submits a CA entry with associated questions, the editorial team reviews both the factual accuracy of the summary and the quality of the questions in a single review pass rather than routing them through separate queues. If the editorial team identifies a factual error in the summary, all linked questions are automatically held from approval until the summary is corrected, because questions derived from incorrect source material are likely to contain the same errors. Approved CA entries are immediately indexed and surfaced in the student-facing daily current affairs feed, organized by date and category, with the partner's name displayed as the contributor attribution. High-performing CA partners (defined as those with a 90 percent or higher approval rate and at least 30 entries per month) are eligible for the "Verified CA Contributor" badge, which increases the visibility of their entries and provides a revenue-share bonus on student engagement with their content.
