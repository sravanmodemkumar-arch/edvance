## Overview

The Study Material Upload module enables content partners to contribute structured notes, chapter PDFs, and HTML-based study guides that map directly to EduForge's syllabus taxonomy. Dr. Venkat Rao uses this feature to upload his comprehensive quantitative aptitude notes — organized by chapter (Number Systems, Algebra, Geometry, etc.) — which are then mapped to specific exam syllabi (APPSC Group 2, SSC CGL, IBPS PO). Each uploaded document is linked to a subject, topic, and optionally sub-topics, enabling students to discover relevant material alongside practice questions.

## Business Rules

1. Uploaded PDF files must not exceed 25 MB in size and 200 pages in length, which accommodates comprehensive chapter-level notes while preventing partners from uploading entire textbooks that would create licensing and copyright complications. The system runs an automated text extraction pass (using Apache PDFBox) on every uploaded PDF to verify that it contains selectable text rather than scanned images, because text-searchable PDFs enable full-text search indexing, accessibility features for screen readers, and plagiarism detection via cross-referencing against EduForge's existing content library and public domain academic databases. If a PDF consists primarily of scanned images (less than 10 percent extractable text by page count), the partner receives a warning recommending OCR processing before upload, though the upload is still accepted with a reduced search discoverability score. Dr. Venkat Rao's notes are always prepared in LaTeX-compiled PDFs which produce fully searchable text, earning them higher visibility in student search results.

2. Every study material must be mapped to at least one exam, one subject, and one topic from EduForge's master syllabus taxonomy before it can be submitted for review. The chapter-level mapping is optional but strongly encouraged because it enables the platform to surface specific chapter ranges when a student is studying a particular sub-topic, rather than requiring them to navigate through the entire document. The system attempts automatic chapter detection by parsing PDF bookmarks, heading structures (H1/H2 tags in tagged PDFs), and page-level text analysis for common chapter indicators such as "Chapter X" or numbered section headers. When auto-detection produces results, the partner reviews and corrects the chapter boundaries on the mapping screen; when it fails (typically for PDFs without structural tagging), the partner must define chapters manually by entering page ranges. The syllabus alignment preview shows which sub-topics under the selected topic are covered and which are missing, helping partners identify content gaps they could fill with subsequent uploads.

3. Study materials progress through a four-stage lifecycle: Draft (partner is still editing or mapping), Submitted (in the editorial review queue), Approved (visible to students on the platform), and Archived (removed from active visibility but retained for historical access). The editorial team reviews submitted materials for content accuracy, formatting quality, alignment with the claimed syllabus mapping, and absence of copyrighted third-party content. Review turnaround is targeted at 72 hours for materials under 50 pages and 120 hours for longer documents. If the editorial team requests revisions, the material returns to Draft status with annotated feedback comments pinned to specific pages, and the partner can view these annotations overlaid on the PDF viewer within the portal. A material that has been approved and is accumulating student engagement (views, downloads, ratings) cannot be deleted by the partner; it can only be archived, which removes it from new student discovery while preserving access for students who previously bookmarked it.

4. The platform computes a syllabus coverage metric for each content partner, broken down by exam and subject, which represents the percentage of syllabus sub-topics for which the partner has at least one approved study material. This metric is displayed on the partner dashboard and factored into the partner's visibility ranking in the EduForge content marketplace. For Dr. Venkat Rao, his Quantitative Aptitude coverage across APPSC Group 2 stands at 85 percent (with Surds and Indices being a notable gap), while his Reasoning coverage is at 40 percent since he has only uploaded two reasoning modules so far. The coverage metric also drives automated suggestions sent via email and in-app notification, alerting partners to high-demand topics where student activity is strong but content availability is low, which helps EduForge organically grow its content library in areas of genuine student need rather than accumulating redundant material on already well-covered topics.
