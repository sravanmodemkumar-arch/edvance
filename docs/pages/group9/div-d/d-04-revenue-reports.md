## 5. Business Rules

- TDS is deducted at source by EduForge on every payout as mandated by the Income Tax Act, 1961; for individual content partners like Dr. Venkat Rao, TDS is deducted at 10% under Section 194J (fees for professional or technical services) because content creation for educational platforms qualifies as professional services; for company or LLP partners like Disha Publications, TDS is deducted at 2% under Section 194C (payment to contractors) when the content licensing arrangement is structured as a contract; if a partner does not furnish their PAN, TDS is deducted at the higher rate of 20% as per Section 206AA, and the system blocks payout processing until PAN is provided and verified against the NSDL/UTIITSL database; EduForge deposits the deducted TDS with the government within the prescribed timeline (7th of the following month) and files quarterly TDS returns in Form 26Q.

- Form 16A certificates are generated quarterly and made available on the partner's dashboard within 15 days of the quarter-end (by 15th July, 15th October, 15th January, and 15th June for Q1 through Q4 respectively); the certificate contains the deductor's TAN (DELE12345F), the deductee's PAN, the amount paid, TDS deducted, and the challan details; partners must cross-verify the TDS credit reflected in their Form 26AS on the TRACES portal (tdscpc.gov.in) because discrepancies between Form 16A and Form 26AS can lead to demand notices during income tax processing; if a partner reports a mismatch (e.g., TDS deducted but not reflected in 26AS), the EduForge finance team investigates the challan deposit and provides rectification within 30 days.

- GST invoicing is required only for partners who are registered under GST (typically companies and LLPs with turnover above Rs. 20 lakh, or Rs. 10 lakh for special category states); individual partners like Dr. Venkat Rao whose annual earnings of Rs. 4,55,850 fall below the GST threshold are not required to charge GST and their payouts are processed without GST; for Disha Publications with GSTIN 07AABCD1234E1Z5, the platform provides an invoice drafting tool where the partner can generate a compliant tax invoice with the correct SAC code (998393 for educational support services), applicable GST rate (18% split as 9% CGST + 9% SGST for intra-state or 18% IGST for inter-state supply), and EduForge's GSTIN as the recipient; reverse charge mechanism is not applicable because the service provider (content partner) is GST-registered.

- The annual earnings statement serves as the primary document for the partner's income tax return filing; the statement covers the Indian financial year from April to March and includes a month-by-month breakdown of gross revenue, commission deducted, net revenue, TDS deducted, and actual amount paid out; the statement reconciles exactly with the sum of all monthly invoices and the quarterly TDS certificates — any rounding differences are adjusted in the March settlement to ensure the annual total matches to the last rupee; partners can download the statement in PDF (for filing) or Excel (for their own accounting) format, and the statement includes a disclaimer that it is generated by EduForge's system and the partner should verify all figures with their chartered accountant before filing returns.
