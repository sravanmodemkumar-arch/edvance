## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/summary/` | Rating summary and distribution for partner's content |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/question/{question_id}/` | Question-level ratings, comments, and error reports |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/error-reports/` | List all error reports (filterable by status, exam, topic) |
| 4 | `POST` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/error-reports/{report_id}/resolve/` | Submit resolution for an error report |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/comments/` | List all student comments with pagination |
| 6 | `POST` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/comments/{comment_id}/reply/` | Partner reply to a student comment |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/explanation-quality/` | Explanation quality ratings breakdown |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/content-partner/analytics/feedback/export/` | Export feedback data as CSV or PDF |

## 5. Business Rules

- Student ratings are collected at the question level immediately after a student submits their answer in a mock test or practice session, using a non-intrusive single-tap star rating (1 to 5) with an optional free-text comment field limited to 500 characters; the rating prompt appears only after the student has viewed the explanation, ensuring the rating reflects both question quality and explanation quality; to prevent rating manipulation, each student can rate a specific question only once (subsequent attempts on the same question do not trigger a new rating prompt), and ratings from students who spent less than 5 seconds on the question (indicating random clicking or skipping) are excluded from the partner's aggregate score; Dr. Venkat Rao's 4.1/5 average rating across 28,450 monthly feedback submissions is computed using a Bayesian weighted average that requires at least 30 ratings per question before the individual question rating is displayed to the partner, preventing volatile scores from low-sample questions from affecting the partner's perceived quality.

- Error reports follow a strict SLA-driven workflow where the partner must respond within 48 hours of the first student report for a given question; if 3 or more students independently report the same question within a 72-hour window, the error report is automatically escalated to "High Priority" status and the response deadline is shortened to 24 hours; Dr. Venkat Rao's Q#6210 with 7 reports in 8 hours is a clear High Priority case; if the partner fails to respond within the SLA deadline, the question is automatically suspended from all live test generation pools until the partner addresses the report, and the Content Team is notified; confirmed errors where the answer key is corrected trigger an automatic retroactive marks credit for all students who selected the newly-correct answer in scored mock tests taken within the last 90 days, with an in-app notification sent to each affected student explaining the correction and their updated score.

- Partner replies to student comments are visible to all students who rated or commented on that question, creating a transparent feedback loop that encourages partners to maintain explanation quality; however, partner replies are moderated by an automated content filter that checks for profanity, dismissive language, and personally identifiable information before publishing; partners cannot delete student comments or ratings — they can only respond to them or flag a comment as "abusive/spam" for Content Team review; the comment thread for each question is capped at 50 visible comments (sorted by most helpful, determined by other students' upvotes), and partners can pin one "official response" to the top of the thread; Dr. Venkat Rao's proactive responses (such as adding shortcut methods based on Ravi K.'s feedback on Q#3847) are tracked as "partner engagement events" that positively contribute to his quality score, specifically the error report resolution rate component.

- Explanation quality is tracked as a separate metric from the overall question rating because EduForge's pedagogical model treats the explanation as the primary learning moment — students learn more from reviewing a well-explained wrong answer than from simply seeing "Correct!" on a right answer; the explanation quality metric measures four dimensions: helpfulness (did the student understand after reading), completeness (were all steps shown), accuracy (were there errors in the explanation), and availability (was an explanation provided at all); Dr. Venkat Rao's 4.3/5 explanation quality score and 78.4% "helpful" rate are above pool average (3.6/5 and 62.1% respectively), reflecting his practice of providing both the standard method and shortcut approaches; however, 10.3% of his questions show "No explanation provided" — these are older questions uploaded before EduForge mandated explanations, and the system flags them in a separate "Missing Explanations" queue with a recommendation to add explanations for all questions with more than 100 attempts to maximise learning impact.
