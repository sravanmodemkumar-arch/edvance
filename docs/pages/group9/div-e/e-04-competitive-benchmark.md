## 5. Business Rules

- Partner rankings within each exam category are computed daily at 02:00 IST using the quality score algorithm, and the leaderboard positions shown to a partner always use anonymised labels ("Anonymous Partner #1", "Anonymous Partner #2") for all other partners to prevent competitive intelligence gathering between content suppliers; Dr. Venkat Rao can see that he is ranked #3 of 45 partners in SSC CGL Tier 1 Quant with a score of 87/100, and that the #1 partner has 92/100 and #2 has 89/100, but he cannot determine the identity of those partners; the EduForge Content Team has access to the de-anonymised leaderboard for partner success management purposes, and may share aggregate category insights (such as "the top 5 SSC Quant partners all have median discrimination above 0.80") in quarterly partner newsletters to encourage quality improvement without revealing individual identities; ranking computation requires a minimum of 10 active partners per exam category — categories with fewer than 10 partners display "Ranking unavailable — insufficient peers" to prevent trivial de-anonymisation.

- The "Top Contributor" badge is the most impactful recognition in the partner programme because it directly unlocks the premium revenue share tier (22% vs the standard 18% of net revenue generated from the partner's content), representing a potential income increase of over 22% for high-volume partners; the badge is awarded when a partner simultaneously meets all three criteria — quality score above 85, more than 5,000 active questions, and a review pass rate above 90% — and is valid for 90 days from the award date; if any criterion drops below the threshold during the 90-day validity period, the partner receives a 14-day grace period with a warning notification before the badge is revoked; Dr. Venkat Rao's badge was awarded on 15 January 2026 and is valid through 14 April 2026, at which point the system will automatically re-evaluate all three criteria and either renew the badge for another 90 days or initiate the grace period; the revenue share tier change (premium to standard or vice versa) takes effect from the first day of the month following the badge status change, not retroactively.

- The improvement roadmap is generated by a rule-based recommendation engine that analyses the gap between the partner's current quality score and the score of the #1 partner in their primary exam category, then decomposes the gap into actionable items ranked by effort-to-impact ratio; for Dr. Venkat Rao, the 5-point gap to #1 in SSC CGL Tier 1 Quant is decomposed into four actionable items — resolving pending error reports (estimated +1.5 weighted points, 2-3 hours effort), adding missing explanations (+1.0 point, 8-10 days), recalibrating low-discrimination questions (+1.5 points, 4-6 weeks), and improving topic-level skip rates (+0.5 points, ongoing); the roadmap is refreshed weekly and adjusts dynamically as the partner completes items or as the target score changes due to other partners' improvements; the roadmap never reveals specific information about what the #1 partner does differently — it only identifies areas where the current partner's own metrics are below optimal levels, ensuring the competitive benchmarking drives self-improvement rather than imitation.

- Pool comparison metrics shown on the benchmarking page are computed as anonymised aggregates across all active partners contributing content to the same exam category and subject area, with statistical safeguards to prevent reverse-engineering of individual partner performance; the minimum cohort size for displaying a pool average is 10 partners — if an exam category has fewer than 10 active partners (for example, a niche state PSC exam), the pool average is computed at the broader subject level instead (all Quant partners regardless of exam) with a disclaimer noting the broader comparison base; pool averages are computed using trimmed means (excluding the top and bottom 5% of partner scores) to prevent outliers from skewing the benchmark, and are updated weekly alongside the IRT recalculation pipeline; Dr. Venkat Rao's SSC CGL Tier 1 Quant pool average of 72/100 represents the trimmed mean of 45 partners, while the IBPS PO Prelims Reasoning pool average of 74/100 represents 38 partners, providing meaningful benchmarks that he can use to identify his relative strengths (SSC Quant, where he exceeds pool by 15 points) and improvement areas (IBPS Reasoning, where his lead is only 7 points).
