## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/live/{child_id}` | Get live GPS coordinates, speed, ETA, and route progress for the child's assigned bus |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/route/{route_id}` | Retrieve full route details including stops, schedule, assigned vehicle, and staff |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/driver/{route_id}` | Get driver and conductor details (name, phone — last 5 digits masked, DL validity) |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/history/{child_id}` | Fetch boarding/alighting log for the child; supports date range filters |
| 5 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/alert-prefs` | Update parent's alert preferences (proximity, boarding, delay, deviation) |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/stops/{route_id}` | List all stops on a route with scheduled times and student counts |
| 7 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/report-issue` | Report a transport issue (delay, safety concern, misbehaviour) to the transport coordinator |
| 8 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/transport/change-stop` | Request a pickup/drop stop change; requires transport coordinator approval |

## 5. Business Rules

- **BR-01 — Real-Time GPS Refresh and Accuracy Mandate:** The school bus GPS tracking system must refresh the bus location on the parent's map every 10 seconds during active trips, using the GPS device installed on the vehicle paired with GSM triangulation as a fallback when the bus enters areas with poor satellite reception (common in congested lanes in Vijayawada's old city near Governorpet). The Transport Coordinator at the institution must ensure that every bus has a functioning GPS device with an active SIM card and that the device IMEI is registered in the EduForge fleet management module. If the GPS signal is lost for more than 3 consecutive minutes, the system must automatically push a notification to all parents on that route stating "Bus VJA-14 location temporarily unavailable — last known position: Governorpet at 7:18 AM" and simultaneously alert the transport coordinator to contact the driver. The system must retain GPS breadcrumb data for a minimum of 90 days for audit and dispute resolution purposes, and this data must be stored in compliance with the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011.

- **BR-02 — RFID-Based Boarding Verification and Parent Notification:** Each student assigned to the school bus service must be issued an RFID-enabled ID card that is tapped against the reader installed at the bus door upon boarding and alighting. When Priya Kumar taps her RFID card at the Labbipet Bus Stop at 7:13 AM, the system must immediately send a push notification and WhatsApp message to Mrs. Lakshmi Devi confirming "Priya boarded Bus VJA-14 at Labbipet at 7:13 AM." If a student who is expected to board at a particular stop does not tap within 5 minutes of the bus departing that stop, the system generates a "Did Not Board" alert to the parent and the transport coordinator. The parent must then either confirm the absence (marking a reason such as leave, self-transport, or holiday) or escalate to the institution. If no parent response is received within 15 minutes, the system escalates automatically to the class teacher. This protocol is designed to prevent any child from being inadvertently left behind, a critical safety requirement per CBSE and state education board transport safety guidelines.

- **BR-03 — Route Deviation and Delay Alert Protocol:** The system must continuously compare the bus's actual GPS trajectory against the pre-defined geofenced route corridor (a 200-metre buffer around the designated path). If the bus deviates beyond this corridor for more than 500 metres or 2 minutes, whichever comes first, an automatic route deviation alert must be sent to all parents on the route, the transport coordinator, and the institution's safety officer. For delays, the system must calculate ETA dynamically using real-time traffic data from Google Maps API or MapMyIndia API (preferred for Indian road network accuracy). If the bus is projected to arrive at any stop more than 10 minutes later than the scheduled time, a delay notification must be pushed to all affected parents downstream with the revised ETA. In Vijayawada, common delay factors include Krishna River bridge traffic, VIP movements on Bandar Road, and seasonal flooding in low-lying areas during October-November; the transport coordinator can pre-configure known delay zones so the system adjusts base ETA calculations during those periods.

- **BR-04 — Driver Credential Verification and Speed Governance:** Before any driver is assigned to a school bus route in the EduForge system, the transport coordinator must upload the driver's commercial driving licence (issued by the AP Transport Department), police verification certificate (not older than 6 months), and medical fitness certificate. The system must validate that the driving licence has the "HTV-Transport Vehicle" endorsement and is not expired. During active trips, the GPS device reports vehicle speed every 10 seconds, and if the bus exceeds 40 km/h within municipal limits or 60 km/h on highways (as per Supreme Court guidelines for school buses in India), the system must log a speed violation event, send an immediate alert to the transport coordinator, and flag the incident in the driver's safety record. Parents can view the driver's safety score (based on speed compliance, on-time record, and zero-incident streak) on the route details screen. If a driver accumulates 3 or more speed violations in a calendar month, the transport coordinator receives a mandatory review alert, and the driver's profile is flagged for reassignment pending review.
