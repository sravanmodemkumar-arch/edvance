## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/profile/{child_id}` | Retrieve child's complete health profile — blood group, allergies, conditions, ABHA linkage |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/vaccinations/{child_id}` | List all vaccination records (completed + due) with certificate URLs |
| 3 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/vaccinations/upload` | Upload a vaccination certificate (PDF/image); triggers OCR extraction and medical officer review |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/infirmary/{child_id}` | Fetch infirmary visit log with doctor notes, treatment, and parent notification status |
| 5 | `PUT` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/allergies/{child_id}` | Add or update allergy information; notifies medical officer and hostel warden |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/insurance/{child_id}` | Retrieve group mediclaim policy details and claim history |
| 7 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/acknowledge/{visit_id}` | Parent acknowledges receipt of infirmary notification; required for audit trail |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/health/nearby-hospitals/{institution_id}` | List nearest government and empanelled private hospitals with distance and contact |

## 5. Business Rules

- **BR-01 — Mandatory Allergy Disclosure and Cross-System Propagation:** When a parent registers or updates an allergy record for their child, the system must immediately propagate this information to all relevant subsystems within the institution: the hostel mess kitchen display (so that cooks are aware of food allergies), the institution medical centre's patient record, and the sports department (for allergies triggered by physical exertion or outdoor exposure). For Ravi Kumar's Penicillin allergy flagged as HIGH severity, the system must ensure that the GCEH infirmary's prescription module blocks any attempt to prescribe Penicillin-class antibiotics (Amoxicillin, Ampicillin, etc.) and displays a prominent red warning to the attending doctor. The parent must upload supporting medical documentation (allergy test report or doctor's letter) within 30 days of declaring a HIGH severity allergy, failing which the allergy remains flagged but is marked as "Unverified" in the doctor's view. All allergy data is classified as Sensitive Personal Data under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and must be encrypted at rest using AES-256 and accessible only to authorized medical personnel, the hostel warden, and the parent.

- **BR-02 — Vaccination Compliance for Hostel Residents:** As per AICTE guidelines for residential engineering colleges, all hostel residents must have up-to-date vaccination records for COVID-19 (minimum 2 doses), Hepatitis B (complete 3-dose series), and any region-specific vaccines recommended by the state health department. The system must check vaccination compliance at three trigger points: (a) at the time of hostel admission, (b) at the start of each academic year, and (c) whenever a new vaccine recommendation is issued by the institution's medical officer. If a student's vaccination record is incomplete, the system generates a compliance alert to the parent with a 30-day remediation window, during which the parent must either upload the missing vaccination certificate or provide a medical exemption letter from a registered medical practitioner. If compliance is not met within 30 days, the hostel warden receives an escalation alert, and the student's hostel allotment is flagged for review by the Dean of Student Welfare. The system supports auto-verification of COVID vaccination status by pulling data from the CoWIN portal via the student's Aadhaar-linked ABHA ID, eliminating the need for manual certificate upload in most cases.

- **BR-03 — Infirmary Visit Notification and Parent Acknowledgement:** Every time a student visits the institution's medical centre or infirmary, the attending doctor must log the visit in the EduForge system within 15 minutes of the consultation. Upon logging, the system must immediately notify the parent via push notification, SMS, and WhatsApp (in the parent's preferred language) with details including the complaint, preliminary diagnosis, treatment administered, and whether the student was referred to an external hospital. For hostel students like Ravi Kumar, the notification also goes to the hostel warden. The parent must acknowledge receipt of the notification within 4 hours by tapping "Acknowledged" in the app or replying "OK" to the WhatsApp message. If the parent does not acknowledge within 4 hours, the system sends a reminder notification and simultaneously places an automated voice call to the parent's registered mobile number. If the visit involves a referral to an external hospital, an injury, or any condition requiring more than 24 hours of bed rest, the notification is classified as "Critical" and the parent is called immediately via the automated voice system, with a fallback to the emergency contact if the primary parent does not answer within 3 attempts.

- **BR-04 — Health Data Privacy and ABHA Integration:** All health and medical records stored in the EduForge system are classified as Sensitive Personal Data Information (SPDI) under the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011, and as Health Data under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023. Access to a child's health records is restricted exclusively to the parent, the institution's designated medical officer, and the hostel warden (for hostel residents only). No teacher, administrative staff, or other parent may access these records. The system integrates with India's Ayushman Bharat Health Account (ABHA) framework, allowing parents to optionally link their child's ABHA ID so that vaccination records from CoWIN, prescriptions from empanelled hospitals, and lab reports from ABHA-participating diagnostic centres can be auto-imported into the student's health profile. When a parent chooses to delink or delete health records, the system must comply within 72 hours and issue a data deletion confirmation, retaining only anonymised aggregate data for institutional health analytics (such as "percentage of hostel students vaccinated for Hepatitis B") as permitted under the DPDP Act's research exemption.
