## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/contacts/{child_id}` | Retrieve the child's registered emergency contacts (parent, local guardian) |
| 2 | `PUT` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/contacts/{child_id}` | Add, update, or remove emergency contacts; validates phone numbers via OTP |
| 3 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/sos/{child_id}` | Trigger SOS alert — sends simultaneous notifications to parent, warden, safety officer, and security |
| 4 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/report-incident` | Submit a safety incident report with type, severity, description, and optional attachments |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/incidents/{child_id}` | List all incidents reported by parent or involving the child, with status and resolution |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/compliance/{institution_id}` | Retrieve institution safety compliance dashboard (AICTE/UGC requirements) |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/helplines/{institution_id}` | Get institution-specific and national emergency helpline numbers |
| 8 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/welfare/emergency/sos-test/{child_id}` | Trigger a silent SOS test to verify all notification channels are working; does not alert security |

## 5. Business Rules

- **BR-01 — SOS One-Tap Alert with Simultaneous Multi-Channel Notification:** When a parent activates the SOS button (tap and hold for 3 seconds to prevent accidental triggers), the system must simultaneously initiate the following actions within 5 seconds: (a) place an automated voice call to the hostel warden with a pre-recorded message stating the student's name, hostel block, room number, and last known campus location; (b) send a push notification and SMS to the institution's safety officer with the same details; (c) trigger an alert on the GCEH security control room dashboard that auto-zooms the CCTV feed to the nearest camera to the student's last known Wi-Fi access point or GPS location; and (d) send a confirmation push notification to the parent confirming that all alerts have been dispatched. If the SOS was triggered by the student from their own app (rather than the parent), the parent receives an immediate call from the system's automated dialler informing them of the emergency. The SOS event is logged with a unique incident ID (e.g., SOS-GCEH-2026-0034) and cannot be deleted or modified by any user; it is immutable for legal and audit purposes. False SOS alerts must still be logged but can be annotated as "False Alarm" by the safety officer after investigation, and students who trigger more than 2 confirmed false alarms in a semester receive a counselling session rather than punitive action.

- **BR-02 — Anti-Ragging Compliance and Mandatory Undertaking:** As mandated by the UGC Regulations on Curbing the Menace of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions (2009) and AICTE directives, every student and their parent must submit a signed anti-ragging undertaking at the beginning of each academic year. The EduForge system must generate the undertaking form pre-filled with the student's and parent's details, present it for digital signature (Aadhaar e-Sign or OTP-based consent), and store the signed document as a tamper-proof PDF with a timestamp and digital certificate. If either the student or the parent has not submitted the undertaking within 15 days of the academic year start date, the system must block the student's hostel access card and send daily reminders to both the parent and the student until compliance is achieved. Any ragging-related incident reported through the portal (by parent, student, or anonymously) must be auto-escalated to the institution's Anti-Ragging Committee and simultaneously reported to the UGC's anti-ragging portal (antiragging.in) via API integration. The institution must resolve ragging complaints within 7 days as per UGC guidelines, and the parent must be notified of the resolution. The national anti-ragging helpline number (1800-180-5522) must be prominently displayed on every page of the welfare module and on the SOS screen.

- **BR-03 — Incident Response SLA and Escalation Matrix:** Every safety incident reported through the portal must follow a defined SLA based on its severity classification. Critical incidents (medical emergency, ragging, fire, assault) must receive a first response from the safety officer within 15 minutes and must be escalated to the Principal and the Dean of Student Welfare within 30 minutes; the parent must receive a status update within 1 hour. High severity incidents (theft, harassment, major infrastructure failure) must receive a first response within 2 hours and resolution within 48 hours. Medium severity incidents (minor maintenance safety hazards, general complaints) must receive a first response within 24 hours and resolution within 7 working days. Low severity incidents (suggestions, minor concerns) must be acknowledged within 48 hours. If any SLA is breached, the system automatically escalates the incident one level up in the hierarchy (safety officer to dean, dean to principal, principal to the institution's managing committee) and sends an escalation notification to the parent. The parent can view the complete incident lifecycle including assignment, status changes, and resolution notes. All incident data must be retained for a minimum of 5 years beyond the student's graduation date, as it may be required for legal proceedings, accreditation audits, or NAAC assessments.

- **BR-04 — Local Guardian Registration for Outstation Students:** For students residing in hostels who are from a different city than the institution (such as Ravi Kumar from Vijayawada studying at GCEH in Hyderabad), the system must mandate the registration of at least one local guardian or emergency contact who resides within 15 kilometres of the campus. The local guardian's details (name, relationship, address, mobile number, Aadhaar — optional) must be verified by the parent and acknowledged by the local guardian via an OTP-based consent flow. The local guardian's phone number is contacted as the third-priority number in any SOS or emergency event, after the primary and secondary parent contacts. If the institution's safety officer or warden is unable to reach the parent within 30 minutes during a critical incident, the system must automatically contact the local guardian as the on-ground emergency responder. The local guardian registration must be renewed at the start of each academic year, and if the parent does not update or re-confirm the local guardian within 30 days of the new academic year, the hostel warden receives a compliance alert. In cities like Hyderabad, where many engineering college students come from Andhra Pradesh districts (Vijayawada, Guntur, Tirupati, Visakhapatnam), having a verified local guardian in Hyderabad is a critical safety requirement that many institutions enforce informally; EduForge formalises this into a trackable, auditable digital process.
