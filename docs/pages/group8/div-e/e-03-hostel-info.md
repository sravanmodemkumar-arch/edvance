## 5. Business Rules

- **BR-01 — Parent Approval Mandatory for All Hostel Exits:** As per AICTE guidelines for engineering college hostels and the institution's in-loco-parentis responsibility, no hostel student may leave the campus for any leave or outing unless the parent or guardian has explicitly approved the request through the EduForge Parents Portal. When Ravi Kumar submits a leave request from his student app, the system must immediately send a push notification, SMS, and WhatsApp message to Mrs. Lakshmi Devi with full details (dates, reason, destination, travel mode). The parent must tap "Approve" or "Reject" in the app, or reply "APPROVE LV-2026-0412" via WhatsApp. Only after receiving parent approval does the system update the request status and notify the hostel warden to authorize gate exit. For local outings (within city, same-day return before curfew), the warden may grant auto-approval for students with no disciplinary record, but the parent must still be notified within 5 minutes of the student exiting the gate. If the parent does not respond to a leave request within 12 hours, the system sends a reminder every 4 hours up to a maximum of 3 reminders, after which the request is auto-escalated to the chief warden who may contact the parent directly by phone.

- **BR-02 — Biometric Gate Logging and Curfew Enforcement:** Every hostel student must tap their biometric (fingerprint) or RFID card at the campus gate upon exit and entry. The system must log the exact timestamp, gate location (Main Gate / Side Gate), and whether the exit was pre-approved. If a student does not return to the hostel before the curfew time (9:30 PM on weekdays, 10:00 PM on weekends and holidays), the system must automatically trigger a "Late Return" alert to the parent and the floor warden at curfew + 10 minutes. If the student has not returned by curfew + 30 minutes, the alert escalates to the chief warden and the Dean of Student Welfare. For students who were on approved leave and are returning via bus or train, the system allows a configurable grace period of up to 60 minutes if the parent provides advance notice of delayed arrival. All gate log data must be retained for the full duration of the student's enrollment plus 1 year beyond graduation, as this data may be required for disciplinary proceedings, insurance claims, or parental disputes. The biometric system must have a battery backup sufficient for 4 hours of operation to ensure logging continuity during power outages, which are common in parts of Hyderabad during monsoon season.

- **BR-03 — Mess Menu Transparency and Dietary Accommodation:** The hostel mess menu must be published on the Parents Portal at least one week in advance, and any changes to the published menu (due to supply shortages, festival specials, or student feedback) must trigger a notification to all parents of hostel residents. The system must display the full weekly menu with meal timings, vegetarian and non-vegetarian options, and highlight allergens where applicable (e.g., "contains peanuts" for peanut chutney, "contains dairy" for paneer dishes). Parents of students with documented allergies (synced from the Health Records module E-02) must receive a specific alert if any menu item contains a declared allergen. The mess committee, which includes elected student representatives, can update the menu through the institution admin portal, and each menu change must go through an approval workflow signed off by the chief warden. Students and parents can rate each meal on a 5-point scale, and if the average rating for any meal falls below 2.5 stars across 50 or more ratings, the system flags it for review by the mess committee. Monthly mess expenditure reports showing per-student cost, wastage estimates, and vendor payments must be accessible to parents for transparency, as hostel mess charges of Rs.36,000 per year constitute a significant portion of the hostel fees.

- **BR-04 — Room Allotment Transparency and Complaint Mechanism:** The hostel room allotment details displayed to parents must include the room number, floor, wing, sharing type (single, 2-sharing, 3-sharing), names of roommates (first name and department only, no personal contact details for privacy), and a list of in-room amenities. If a parent or student wishes to request a room change (due to compatibility issues, medical reasons such as a ground-floor requirement for a student with mobility limitations, or maintenance issues like non-functional fans or water leakage), they can submit a Room Change Request through the portal. The request must include a reason category (medical, maintenance, personal, or other) and a brief description. The system routes the request to the floor warden for initial assessment, then to the chief warden for approval. Medical-category requests must be processed within 48 hours and require supporting documentation from the institution medical officer. Non-medical requests are processed in the order received with a target turnaround of 7 working days. Parents can track the status of their request in real time, and the system sends status update notifications at each stage (received, under review, approved/rejected, room change completed). The institution must maintain a minimum occupancy standard of 40 square feet per student in shared rooms, as recommended by UGC hostel guidelines, and room allotment data must reflect this compliance.
