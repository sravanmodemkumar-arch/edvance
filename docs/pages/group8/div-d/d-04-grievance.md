  SLA deadline:   07 April 2026 (7 days remaining)

  ── ESCALATION LEVELS ───────────────────────────────────────────

  LEVEL 1 (Current):  Hostel Warden / Class Teacher / Advisor
                       Response SLA: 48 hours
                       Resolution SLA: 7 days
                       Status: ACTIVE — responded within SLA

  LEVEL 2 (Manual):   HOD / Vice-Principal / Dean of Student Affairs
                       Trigger: Parent clicks "Escalate" OR
                                Level 1 SLA breached
                       Response SLA: 24 hours
                       Resolution SLA: 5 days from escalation

  LEVEL 3 (Manual):   Principal / Director
                       Trigger: Parent clicks "Escalate" after Level 2 OR
                                Level 2 SLA breached
                       Response SLA: 12 hours
                       Resolution SLA: 3 days from escalation

  LEVEL 4 (Auto):     EduForge Platform Compliance Team
                       Trigger: Level 3 SLA breached OR
                                grievance unresolved for 30+ days
                       Action: Compliance team contacts institution
                               management directly; may affect
                               institution rating on platform

  ── ESCALATE NOW ────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Escalate to: [Level 2 — HOD / Dean of Student Affairs ▼]

  ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

  NOTE: For Ragging/Harassment grievances, escalation is automatic
  — all levels are notified simultaneously including the Anti-Ragging
  Committee chairperson and the AICTE portal (as per UGC regulations).

## 5. Business Rules

- Ragging and harassment grievances follow a separate fast-track workflow mandated by AICTE and UGC regulations; when a parent selects the "Ragging / Bullying / Harassment" category, the grievance simultaneously notifies the Anti-Ragging Committee chairperson, the principal/director, the dean of student affairs, and the hostel warden (if hostel-related); the parent is offered the option to file anonymously, and the system strips all identifying metadata (IP address, device info, timestamp rounded to the day) from anonymous filings to protect the complainant; the institution must acknowledge within 6 hours and begin investigation within 24 hours as per UGC Regulation 2009; if the institution does not respond within 6 hours, the system auto-escalates to the EduForge Compliance Team and generates a report that can be forwarded to the AICTE anti-ragging portal; this zero-tolerance approach reflects the serious nature of ragging in Indian engineering colleges, where incidents have historically led to tragic outcomes.

- The four-level escalation hierarchy ensures that no grievance falls through the cracks, which is a persistent problem in Indian educational institutions where informal complaints are often ignored; Level 1 handles routine matters (attendance corrections, minor infrastructure issues) at the class teacher or warden level; Level 2 brings in departmental authority (HOD, vice-principal) for matters that require policy decisions or cross-departmental coordination; Level 3 involves the principal or director for serious matters or when lower levels have failed to resolve the issue; Level 4 is the EduForge platform-level escalation, which exists as an accountability mechanism — institutions know that unresolved grievances affect their platform rating and could be flagged in EduForge's institutional quality reports; the parent can manually escalate at any time (they do not have to wait for the SLA to expire), but the system also auto-escalates when SLA deadlines pass without resolution.

- Anonymous grievance filing is available for all categories but is especially important for sensitive matters like faculty misconduct, caste-based discrimination, and favouritism in evaluations; when a grievance is filed anonymously, the investigating authority sees the complaint details and evidence but not the parent or student identity; however, the institution admin (a designated neutral party) retains access to the identity in a sealed record that can only be opened by the principal with documented justification; this prevents misuse of the anonymous system for false complaints while still protecting genuine complainants; the anonymous option displays a clear disclaimer that false complaints with malicious intent may result in action against the complainant if the sealed identity is opened during investigation, which deters frivolous filings without discouraging genuine ones.

- Every resolved grievance requires a parent satisfaction rating (1-5 stars) and an optional text review before the ticket is closed; if the parent rates the resolution 1 or 2 stars, the grievance is not closed but instead moves to a "Disputed Resolution" state where the next escalation level reviews the resolution; the institution cannot unilaterally close a grievance without the parent's acknowledgement (the system auto-closes only after 15 days of parent inactivity post-resolution); these satisfaction metrics are aggregated at the institution level and visible to the institution admin as a "Parent Satisfaction Index" in their dashboard; institutions with consistently low satisfaction scores receive improvement recommendations from EduForge's education quality team, and in extreme cases (satisfaction below 2.0 stars across 50+ grievances in a quarter), the institution may be flagged for review, creating a market-driven incentive for institutions to take parent complaints seriously rather than treating them as administrative nuisances.
