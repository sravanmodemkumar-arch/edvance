## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/` | List upcoming and past PTM events for linked children |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/{event_id}/slots/` | Get available time slots for a PTM event |
| 3 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/{event_id}/book/` | Book a specific slot (slot_id, mode, topics, notes) |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/` | List all PTM bookings for the parent |
| 5 | `PATCH` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/{booking_id}/reschedule/` | Reschedule to a different slot |
| 6 | `DELETE` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/{booking_id}/` | Cancel a PTM booking (min 4 hrs before) |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/{booking_id}/notes/` | Get post-meeting notes and action items |
| 8 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/{booking_id}/feedback/` | Submit PTM session feedback and rating |
| 9 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/ptm/bookings/{booking_id}/video-link/` | Get video call link (available 1 hr before) |

## 5. Business Rules

- PTM slot booking replaces the traditional Indian school practice where parents queue for hours on PTM day waiting to meet the class teacher; in a typical Vijayawada junior college with 480 parents per section, unstructured PTMs result in 2-3 hour waits, rushed 2-minute conversations, and parents leaving without meeting the teacher; the slot-based system guarantees each parent a fixed 15-minute window (junior college) or 20-minute window (B.Tech) with the class teacher or class advisor, with pre-selected discussion topics shared with the teacher in advance so the meeting is focused and productive; the system caps slots at the teacher's capacity (typically 20 parents per PTM day per teacher) and waitlisted parents are offered the next available date or a video call alternative.

- Video call PTM is a critical feature for parents whose children study in a different city, which is extremely common in Indian higher education; Mrs. Lakshmi Devi in Vijayawada cannot easily travel 300 km to Hyderabad for a 20-minute meeting with Ravi's class advisor at GCEH; the video call option uses integrated Google Meet or Jitsi, and the meeting link is generated automatically and sent to both parent and teacher one hour before the scheduled time; video PTMs are recorded (with consent from both parties) and the recording is stored for 30 days for reference; institutions can mandate that at least one PTM per year must be in-person for colleges, while junior colleges and schools with local parents may require all PTMs to be in-person.

- Post-PTM meeting notes are entered by the class teacher or class advisor within 24 hours of the meeting and become visible to the parent on the portal; notes include academic summary, attendance status, behavioural observations, and specific action items with deadlines; action items create trackable entries that the system follows up on — for example, if an action item says "Ravi to attend all remaining DAA lab sessions," the system checks Ravi's attendance data before the next PTM and flags whether the action was completed; this follow-through mechanism transforms PTMs from perfunctory rituals into accountability-driven conversations, and the parent can see a historical record of all PTM notes across semesters to track whether issues raised in previous meetings have been resolved.

- PTM scheduling respects Indian calendar constraints and institutional norms; PTMs are always scheduled on Saturdays (working day for most Indian schools and colleges) or during declared PTM days that are holidays for students but working days for faculty; the system prevents scheduling PTMs during exam periods, festival weeks (Dussehra, Sankranti, Ugadi), or summer vacations; if a parent has children in multiple institutions, the system detects scheduling conflicts and warns if two PTMs overlap (for example, Sri Chaitanya PTM on the same Saturday morning as GCEH PTM); cancellation must happen at least 4 hours before the slot, and cancelled slots are immediately released to waitlisted parents with an auto-notification; no-show parents (booked but did not attend or join video call) are flagged, and after two consecutive no-shows, the system restricts their ability to book prime morning slots in future PTMs, giving priority to parents with better attendance records.
