## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/` | List all messages (paginated, filterable by child) |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/{thread_id}/` | Get full conversation thread |
| 3 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/` | Compose and send a new message |
| 4 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/{thread_id}/reply/` | Reply to existing thread |
| 5 | `PATCH` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/{msg_id}/read/` | Mark message as read |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/recipients/` | List available recipients for a child |
| 7 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/{msg_id}/attach/` | Upload attachment to message |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/communicate/messages/unread-count/` | Unread count per child |

## 5. Business Rules

- Parent-to-teacher messaging is scoped strictly by the child's institutional context; a parent can only message faculty, staff, and administrators who are mapped to their linked child's institution, department, and section; for B.Tech students at GCEH, the primary contacts are the class advisor (not class teacher, since engineering colleges use the advisor/mentor system), the HOD, subject faculty, and hostel warden; for junior college students like Priya at Sri Chaitanya, the primary contact is the class teacher who handles all subject coordination, followed by the vice-principal and branch head; this scoping prevents parents from contacting unrelated staff and ensures messages reach the right person without manual routing.

- All messages exchanged on the platform are stored indefinitely and are visible to the institution admin for compliance and safety monitoring; this is necessary because AICTE and state education boards require institutions to maintain records of parent-institution communication, especially regarding attendance warnings, disciplinary matters, and fee disputes; the institution admin can view but not modify message content; parents are informed at signup that messaging is monitored, and a banner in the compose window reminds them of this policy; this transparency protects both parents and teachers in case of disputes.

- Message delivery follows a multi-channel notification strategy to ensure critical communication is not missed; when a teacher sends a message categorized as "Attendance" or "Health/Emergency," the parent receives an in-app notification, an email, and an SMS to their registered mobile number; for general and academic categories, only in-app and email notifications are sent; WhatsApp Business API integration is available as an opt-in channel for institutions that have configured it; SMS is sent in the parent's preferred language (Telugu, Hindi, or English) as set in their profile preferences, ensuring accessibility for parents who may not be comfortable reading English.

- Messaging windows and response SLA enforcement protect both parents and teachers from unreasonable expectations; messages composed by parents between 8 PM and 8 AM are queued and delivered at 8 AM the next day, preventing late-night disturbances to teachers; teachers and class advisors have a 24-hour SLA to respond, while HODs and administrative staff have a 48-hour SLA; if the SLA is breached, the system auto-escalates the message to the next authority level (class advisor to HOD, HOD to Dean); parents can see the SLA countdown on each sent message, and institution admins receive a daily digest of breached SLAs to ensure accountability without micromanagement.
