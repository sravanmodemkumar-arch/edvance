## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/timetable/?date=2026-03-31` | Daily class timetable for a specific date |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/timetable/weekly/` | Full weekly timetable grid for the current week |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/exams/` | Upcoming exam schedule with dates and subjects |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/exams/{exam_id}/` | Detailed exam timetable for a specific exam series |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/holidays/?month=2026-04` | Holiday calendar for the institution for a given month |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/coaching/schedule/` | Coaching centre weekly schedule (live + practice + mocks) |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/overview/` | All children today's schedule summary for the parent |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/schedule/{child_id}/hall-ticket/` | Download hall ticket PDF (when published by institution) |

## 5. Business Rules

- The timetable displayed on the parent portal is a read-only mirror of the institution's master timetable and updates automatically when the institution admin publishes a revised schedule; Indian educational institutions frequently change timetables mid-semester due to faculty availability, exam preparation periods, or remedial classes, and the parent portal must reflect these changes within 5 minutes of publication; when GCEH changes Ravi Kumar's Monday DS Lab from 2:00 PM to 3:00 PM because Dr. Pradeep has a faculty meeting, the updated timetable propagates to Mrs. Lakshmi Devi's portal through a webhook-triggered cache invalidation; the platform maintains a timetable version history so that if a parent disputes an attendance marking ("my child was marked absent but there was no class that day"), the institution can pull up the timetable that was effective on that date; for schools like Sri Chaitanya which operate a 6-day week with 42 periods, the weekly view uses a compact grid format, while for colleges like GCEH with 5-day weeks and longer periods, the layout adapts automatically based on the institution's schedule configuration

- Exam schedules are published by the institution and appear on the parent portal with a configurable lead time; JNTU-H university exams for affiliated colleges like GCEH are published by the university examination branch and imported into EduForge through a data feed that the GCEH admin verifies before publishing to parents; the platform supports three exam schedule categories — internal assessments (controlled by the institution, typically 2 weeks notice), institution-level exams like Sri Chaitanya's quarterly and annual exams (1 month notice), and university or board exams (schedule published by the external body); for each exam, the parent can see the date, time, venue, and hall ticket download link once available; the hall ticket download is a critical feature because Indian university exams require a printed hall ticket for entry, and parents often help their children manage these logistics — the PDF is generated by the institution's exam module and made available through a secure, time-limited download link that expires 48 hours after the last exam date

- Holiday calendars on the parent portal are institution-specific and reflect the actual working calendar declared by each institution, which may differ even within the same city; when Ugadi falls on 10 April 2026, both Sri Chaitanya Junior College and GCEH may declare it a holiday, but Sri Chaitanya might conduct exams on Ram Navami (17 April) while GCEH observes it as a holiday — the parent portal shows the correct calendar for each child's institution without mixing them up; the platform imports state government holiday lists (AP State Government declared holidays for aided institutions, JNTU-H academic calendar for affiliated colleges) as a starting point, and the institution admin can add, remove, or modify holidays based on their management's decisions; Saturday working status is configurable — Sri Chaitanya operates a 6-day week (Saturday is a working day), while GCEH has alternate Saturdays off; this distinction is important for parents planning family activities and is clearly indicated in the weekly timetable view with Saturday periods either populated or marked as "Holiday"

- Coaching centre schedules are displayed alongside formal institution timetables but are visually distinguished to prevent confusion; when Ravi Kumar has GCEH classes from 9 AM to 4 PM and a TopRank Academy live doubt-clearing session at 8 PM on Tuesday, both appear on his combined daily schedule but in separate sections with clear institution branding; the coaching schedule includes three types of activities — self-paced practice (flexible timing, no conflict possible), live sessions (fixed timing, the platform checks for conflicts with the college timetable and flags them), and mock tests (fixed 3-hour windows on weekends); if a coaching mock test is scheduled on the same day as a college internal exam, the platform raises a scheduling conflict alert to the parent, allowing them to coordinate with the child; the coaching schedule also shows the next upcoming mock test date and the countdown ("GATE Mock #15 in 12 days") because parents tracking their child's GATE preparation find this countdown motivating and use it to ensure the child is maintaining study discipline in the run-up to each mock
