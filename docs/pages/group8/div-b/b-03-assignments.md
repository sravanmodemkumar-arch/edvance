## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/pending/` | List of pending and overdue assignments for a child |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/graded/` | Graded assignments with marks and teacher feedback |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/all/?month=2026-03` | All assignments for a month (pending, submitted, graded) |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/summary/` | Semester-level assignment statistics per subject |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/overdue/` | Overdue assignments with late penalty details |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/coaching/practice/` | Coaching practice set tracker and weekly stats |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/{child_id}/coaching/practice/{set_id}/` | Detailed results for a specific practice set |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/assignments/overview/` | All children pending/overdue assignment counts for the parent |

## 5. Business Rules

- The parent portal displays assignment status in four states — pending (assigned but not yet submitted), submitted (uploaded by the student but not yet graded), graded (faculty has evaluated and assigned marks), and overdue (deadline has passed without submission) — and the parent can only view these states, never modify or submit on behalf of the child; this read-only constraint is enforced at the API level where the parent's JWT token carries a role claim of "parent" which the authorization middleware maps to read-only permissions on the assignments resource; the rationale is that assignment submission must originate from the student's authenticated session to maintain academic integrity, and allowing a parent to submit would create ambiguity about whether the student actually completed the work; however, the parent's visibility into overdue assignments serves as a critical accountability mechanism, especially in the Indian family context where parents often check homework completion daily for school-age children like Priya Kumar, and the push notification for overdue items ("Ravi has 1 overdue assignment in Software Engineering — due 3 days ago") ensures the parent is informed even without opening the app

- Late submission penalties are calculated and displayed according to the institution's configured penalty policy, and the parent portal shows the projected marks loss in real-time so that parents can intervene; when Ravi Kumar's Software Engineering case study is 3 days overdue with a penalty of 2 marks per day, the portal shows a running penalty of 6 marks (out of the total 10 marks for the assignment), making it viscerally clear to Mrs. Lakshmi Devi that her son is losing marks every day; the late submission window is also institution-configurable — GCEH allows late submissions up to 7 days after the deadline with a cumulative penalty, while Sri Chaitanya Junior College does not accept late homework at all (status changes from "overdue" to "missed" after 24 hours); the platform supports four penalty models that institutions can choose from — flat deduction (e.g., minus 2 per day), percentage deduction (e.g., minus 10% per day), grace period with cliff (e.g., no penalty for 2 days then 50% deduction), and no late submission; these policies are configured at the subject level, allowing different departments within the same institution to have different rules

- Coaching centre practice sets are tracked differently from formal institution assignments because they are self-paced learning resources rather than graded submissions; when TopRank Academy assigns "Algorithms Practice Set #8" to Ravi Kumar's GATE batch, it carries a recommended deadline rather than a hard deadline, and there is no marks penalty for completing it late; however, the parent portal prominently displays the weekly practice target (8 hours for Ravi's batch) alongside actual practice time (5h 15m this week, which is 65.6% of target) because this engagement metric is what parents of coaching students care about most; the weekly target is configured by the coaching admin based on the exam preparation schedule — a student preparing for GATE 2027 (still 10 months away) might have a 6-hour weekly target, while a student 2 months from the exam would have a 15-hour target; the platform also computes accuracy trends per topic area, so Mrs. Lakshmi Devi can see that Ravi's Algorithms accuracy is only 56–72% compared to 85–90% in Aptitude and Digital Logic, providing actionable insight for focused preparation

- Group assignments for college students display the team composition and individual contribution indicators where the institution has enabled peer assessment; when Ravi Kumar's Database Systems ER Diagram assignment is a team project with Suresh and Keerthi, the parent portal shows the team members' names (but not their marks or personal details, for privacy) and the assignment's collective status; if the institution has enabled the peer review module, each team member rates the contribution of others, and the faculty uses this to adjust individual marks — the parent can see their child's contribution score but not the scores of other team members; this transparency addresses a common concern among Indian parents that their child is "doing all the work" in group projects while others free-ride; the platform does not expose other students' contact information, marks, or submission details to the parent — only the minimum information needed to understand the team context, in compliance with the institution's data sharing policy configured during Group 8 Division A onboarding
