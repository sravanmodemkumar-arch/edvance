## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/latest/` | Latest exam/assessment result for a child |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/exams/` | List of all exams with aggregate scores and ranks |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/exams/{exam_id}/` | Detailed subject-wise result for a specific exam |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/cgpa/` | CGPA/SGPA progression across semesters (college) |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/subjects/{subject_id}/trend/` | Marks trend for a single subject across exams |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/{child_id}/internals/` | Internal assessment breakdown (mid-term, assignments) |

| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/academics/results/overview/` | All children latest results summary for the parent |

## 5. Business Rules

- Result visibility on the parent portal is controlled entirely by the institution admin's publish action; when a faculty member enters marks for Priya Kumar's Quarterly Exam into Sri Chaitanya's system, those marks remain in "draft" status and are invisible to parents until the institution admin (or examination controller) clicks "Publish Results" for that specific exam; this prevents parents from seeing partially entered or unverified data, which is critical in Indian institutions where marks entry errors are common and can cause panic among parents — a single wrong entry showing 12/100 instead of 92/100 for a top student would trigger immediate phone calls, social media complaints, and reputational damage to the institution; the publish workflow also generates a PDF report card that the parent can download, and the institution can configure whether rank information is visible to parents or hidden (some CBSE-affiliated schools hide ranks to reduce competitive pressure, while AP State Board and most coaching centres display ranks prominently)

- CGPA and percentage conversion for JNTU-H affiliated colleges follows the university's official formula where percentage is calculated as (CGPA - 0.75) multiplied by 10, so Ravi Kumar's 7.8 CGPA translates to 70.5%; the parent portal displays both CGPA and the equivalent percentage because many Indian parents, especially those who studied under the marks-based system, do not intuitively understand the 10-point CGPA scale and frequently ask "what percentage did my child get"; the SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) is calculated using the credit-weighted formula prescribed by JNTU-H, and the cumulative CGPA is the credit-weighted average of all SGPAs; when Ravi's Semester 5 is in progress, the system shows an estimated CGPA based on internal marks projections but clearly labels it with an asterisk and "estimated" tag to avoid confusion with the official university result; the platform supports different grading schemes per university — JNTU-H's 10-point scale, Anna University's 10-point scale with different grade boundaries, and VTU's credit system — all configured at the institution level during onboarding

- Coaching mock test results displayed on the parent portal include not only the raw score but also the all-India rank and percentile computed across all students who attempted that specific mock on the TopRank Academy platform; when Ravi Kumar scores 78/100 on GATE Mock #14, his rank of 342 out of 8,400 and percentile of 95.9% give Mrs. Lakshmi Devi a meaningful understanding of where her son stands relative to the competition, which is far more valuable than the raw score alone; the subject-wise breakdown identifies weak areas (Algorithms at 66.7%, OS+CN at 70.0%) so that the parent can have an informed conversation with the child about where to focus; the platform computes these analytics in near-real-time after the mock test window closes using a batch processing job that runs within 15 minutes of test completion; historical mock trends are presented as a line chart showing improvement trajectory, and the parent portal highlights whether the child's trend is "improving," "plateauing," or "declining" based on a 3-test moving average

- The parent portal aggregates results from multiple institutions into a single view but never intermingles data across institutions; Mrs. Lakshmi Devi sees Priya Kumar's Sri Chaitanya results and Ravi Kumar's GCEH results and TopRank coaching results on the same screen, but each result set is clearly attributed to its source institution with the institution's name, logo, and board/university affiliation displayed; this separation is legally important because each institution owns its academic data and has consented to share only specific fields with the parent portal — for example, Sri Chaitanya may allow parents to see marks and ranks but not internal remarks from teachers, while GCEH may share CGPA but not the detailed internal assessment split until after the institution publishes it; the data sharing configuration is managed through a visibility matrix that the institution admin sets up, and the parent portal renders only the fields that the institution has marked as "parent-visible"; if an institution revokes parent access (for example, during a fee dispute), the portal shows a "Results temporarily unavailable — contact institution" message rather than stale cached data
