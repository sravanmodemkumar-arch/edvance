## 5. Business Rules

- Scholarship amounts must be reflected in the parent's fee dashboard as a deduction from gross fees, not as a separate reimbursement, because most Indian government scholarships (including AP ePass) disburse funds directly to the institution rather than to the student or parent; when GCEH receives Rs. 35,000 from the AP Social Welfare Department for Ravi's ePass scholarship, the institution's accounts office credits this against Ravi's fee ledger, and EduForge's parent dashboard shows the reduced net payable amount; however, the timing of disbursement is unpredictable (AP ePass for 2025-26 was sanctioned in September but credited to GCEH only in October), so the parent may need to pay the full fee upfront and receive the scholarship credit as an adjustment in a subsequent instalment; the fee dashboard must clearly show both scenarios — "scholarship applied" when credited and "scholarship sanctioned but not yet disbursed" when approved but pending transfer.

- AP ePass scholarship tracking requires integration with the state government's ePass portal (apepass.cgg.gov.in, operated by CGG — Centre for Good Governance) which exposes a limited status API; EduForge polls this API daily for each child who has an active ePass application, mapping the government's status codes to the seven-step timeline displayed to the parent (application submitted, institution verification, document verification, district approval, state sanction, disbursement, fee adjustment); if the API is unavailable (which happens during peak processing months of August-October), EduForge falls back to the last known status and displays a "last checked" timestamp so the parent knows the data may be stale; the parent can also manually update the status by entering their ePass application number, which triggers an immediate API poll rather than waiting for the daily batch.

- Merit-based concessions from institutions (GCEH merit concession of Rs. 10,000 for CGPA >= 8.5, Sri Chaitanya merit scholarship of Rs. 15,000 for Class 10 marks >= 95%) are managed entirely within the institution's fee structure and are automatically applied to the parent's fee account when the institution's scholarship committee approves them; EduForge does not determine eligibility for institution-level concessions — it only reflects what the institution has approved; however, the platform does surface available concessions that the parent has not yet applied for (such as Sri Chaitanya's early payment discount of Rs. 2,000 for paying the full annual fee before June) because parents often miss these opportunities; the system checks eligibility criteria against available data and shows a "you may be eligible" prompt without making any guarantee, since final approval rests with the institution.

- Scholarship and concession amounts must never result in a negative fee balance or an overpayment refund situation within the EduForge platform; if Ravi's total scholarships (Rs. 35,000 ePass + Rs. 10,000 merit = Rs. 45,000) exceed a single instalment's tuition component (Rs. 42,500 in the first instalment), the excess Rs. 2,500 is carried forward as credit to the next instalment rather than refunded; the system calculates the optimal allocation of scholarship credits across instalments to minimise the parent's out-of-pocket payment at each instalment date; for government scholarships where the disbursement timing is uncertain, the system does not pre-deduct the expected scholarship amount from the due instalment — the parent pays the full amount and the scholarship credit is applied retroactively when funds arrive, because relying on expected scholarship amounts to reduce due instalments has historically led to fee arrears when government disbursements are delayed by months.
