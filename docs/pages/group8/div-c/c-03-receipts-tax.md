## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/receipts/` | List all fee receipts (filterable by child, year, institution) |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/receipts/{receipt_id}/download/` | Download individual receipt as PDF |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/receipts/bulk-download/` | Download all receipts for a period as ZIP |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/tax-certificate/{child_id}/` | Section 80C tuition fee certificate for a child |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/annual-statement/` | Annual fee statement across all children and institutions |
| 6 | `POST` | `/api/v1/parent/fees/annual-statement/email/` | Email annual statement to specified address (for CA) |

## 5. Business Rules

- Fee receipts displayed to the parent are generated by the institution's accounts module, not by EduForge's payment gateway; when a payment succeeds through Razorpay or Paytm PG, EduForge notifies the institution's fee management system, which then generates the official receipt with the institution's GSTIN, receipt serial number, authorized signatory digital signatures, and fee head breakdown; EduForge stores a cached copy of this receipt PDF for quick download but the authoritative document is the institution's version; if the institution regenerates or corrects a receipt (for example, correcting a fee head classification), the cached copy on EduForge is invalidated and re-fetched within 1 hour; the receipt must always display the payment reference from both the gateway and EduForge so that any dispute can be traced across all three systems.

- Section 80C tax certificates require careful separation of tuition fee from other fee heads because only tuition fee paid to a recognised educational institution qualifies for deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961; the system must exclude hostel fees, bus transport fees, exam fees, lab fees, special fees, and coaching subscription charges from the 80C calculation even though they appear on the same receipt; for GCEH, only Rs. 85,000 of the Rs. 2,00,000 total annual payment qualifies; the institution (not EduForge) issues the official 80C certificate because the Income Tax Department requires the certificate to bear the institution's PAN, registration number under the relevant education act, and the authorised signatory's details; EduForge's consolidated 80C statement is explicitly marked as "for reference only — not a legal document" to avoid misuse during tax assessment.

- Annual fee statements must reconcile to the exact rupee across all payment modes and all institutions; the statement aggregates every transaction recorded against the parent's linked children, including partial payments, refunds (if any), and late fees charged; each line item shows the receipt number, fee head, amount, and payment mode so that the parent or their chartered accountant can cross-verify against bank statements; the statement distinguishes between financial year (April to March, relevant for tax) and academic year (June to May, relevant for fee structure), and the same payment may appear in different academic year and financial year contexts; for example, Priya's Q4 payment in March 2026 falls in FY 2025-26 but is for the academic year 2025-26 ending in May.

- Receipt and certificate availability depends on the institution's issuance timeline, which varies by institution type; GCEH issues receipts instantly upon payment confirmation (within 5 minutes) because their accounts module is fully integrated with EduForge's payment callback; Sri Chaitanya issues receipts within 24 hours because their legacy accounts system processes online payments in a nightly batch; TopRank generates receipts immediately as they use EduForge's native billing module; the 80C certificate for Sri Chaitanya is only available after the final quarterly payment (March) because the institution issues a consolidated annual tuition fee certificate rather than per-payment certificates; the platform shows clear status indicators ("Ready", "Available after Mar 2026", "Processing") so the parent knows when to expect each document without contacting the institution's accounts office.
