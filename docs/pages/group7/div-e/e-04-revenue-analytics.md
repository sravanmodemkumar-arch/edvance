## 5. Business Rules

- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) is calculated as the sum of all active student subscriptions at their current monthly price, normalised to a 30-day equivalent; annual subscriptions are divided by 12 to get their monthly contribution (e.g., a student on the All-Access Annual plan at ₹4,999/year contributes ₹416.58 to MRR); free trial students contribute ₹0 to MRR until they convert to a paid plan; MRR is calculated at 23:59 IST on the last day of each month and becomes the official figure for that month; the MRR breakdown separates "New MRR" (first-time subscriptions), "Expansion MRR" (plan upgrades), "Contraction MRR" (plan downgrades), and "Churned MRR" (cancelled or expired subscriptions); this decomposition helps the TSP Admin understand whether growth is driven by acquisition or retention and take targeted action accordingly

- Student Lifetime Value (LTV) is computed using the formula LTV = ARPU x Average Lifespan x Gross Margin, where ARPU is the weighted average monthly revenue across all plans, Average Lifespan is measured from first subscription to final cancellation (reactivations reset the clock), and Gross Margin excludes payment gateway fees and refunds but includes EduForge platform costs as the TSP's cost of goods sold; the LTV calculation updates on the 1st of each month using a rolling 12-month window of data; for TSPs with less than 6 months of history, the system uses industry benchmarks (Indian edtech subscription median lifespan of 6.5 months) with a warning label; Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is self-reported by the TSP Admin through the analytics settings — EduForge does not have visibility into the TSP's marketing spend, so the TSP must manually input or connect their Google Ads and Meta Ads accounts for automated CAC tracking

- Cohort analysis groups students by their subscription start month and tracks what percentage remain active in subsequent months; the retention matrix uses M0 (signup month) as 100% baseline and shows the decay curve; a healthy edtech subscription business in India typically retains 85-90% at M1, 65-70% at M3, and 45-50% at M6; TopRank's M1 retention has improved from 87% to 94% over 12 months, suggesting that onboarding improvements (welcome series, first mock test prompt, study plan setup) are working; the system flags "at-risk" cohorts where M1 drop exceeds 15% and recommends specific interventions — for example, if a June cohort shows 82% M1 retention vs. 92% average, the system alerts the TSP Admin and suggests checking if content for that cohort's target exam was insufficient or if a competitor ran a promotional campaign that month

- Revenue projections use a simple but effective model that combines current student count, historical acquisition rate, current churn rate, and ARPU to forecast MRR for the next 6 months; three scenarios are generated — pessimistic (churn increases by 50%, acquisition drops by 33%), base case (current trends continue), and optimistic (churn decreases by 40%, acquisition increases by 67%); these projections are not financial advice and carry a disclaimer that actual results depend on market conditions, exam calendars (student signups spike 2-3 months before major exams like APPSC Group-1 or IBPS PO), and the TSP's own marketing efforts; the projections are recalculated weekly every Monday at 06:00 IST and the TSP Admin receives a weekly "Revenue Pulse" email with the updated forecast, notable changes, and recommended actions; EduForge's Account Manager for the TSP also receives this report to proactively offer support if projections show declining trends
