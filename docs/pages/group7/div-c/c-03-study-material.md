## 4. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/categories/` | List content categories with counts for the student's exam |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/notes/` | List PDF notes with filters (subject, access tier) |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/notes/{note_id}/` | Get note detail and signed download URL |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/videos/` | List video lectures with progress tracking |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/videos/{video_id}/` | Video detail, chapters, and resume position |
| 6 | `POST` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/videos/{video_id}/progress/` | Update video watch progress and chapter completion |
| 7 | `POST` | `/api/v1/student/study-material/videos/{video_id}/notes/` | Save timestamped personal note on a video |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/current-affairs/{year}/{month}/` | Monthly current affairs capsule with category breakdown |
| 9 | `POST` | `/api/v1/student/current-affairs/{article_id}/bookmark/` | Bookmark a current affairs article for revision |

## 5. Business Rules

- Study material access follows the same subscription tier model as tests; FREE notes and videos are accessible to all registered students — typically 2-3 introductory documents per subject that serve as a teaser; PREMIUM content requires an active quarterly or annual subscription; the TSP admin decides which content is free vs premium through the content CMS (Division B); if a student's subscription expires mid-month, they retain read access to any PDF they have already downloaded (the file is cached locally) but lose streaming access to premium videos and cannot download new PDFs; this grace-on-downloaded-content policy exists because Indian telecom networks are unreliable and students in rural AP may have already downloaded PDFs for offline study during power cuts — revoking those would cause frustration and support tickets
- Video lectures are served through a CDN with tenant-isolated storage buckets; TopRank Academy's 126 videos (82 hours) are stored in the TSP's allocated S3-compatible bucket and served via CloudFront with signed URLs that expire in 4 hours; the video player enforces DRM-lite protection — the signed URL is tied to the student's session token and IP address, so sharing the URL with another student results in a 403 error; the player tracks watch progress at 10-second intervals via the progress API, enabling accurate "resume where you left off" functionality; playback speed options (1x, 1.25x, 1.5x, 2x) are critical for Indian exam prep students who frequently watch lectures at 1.5x to cover more ground; Telugu and English subtitles are supported because TopRank Academy's students are primarily Telugu-medium with varying English proficiency
- Current affairs capsules are a hybrid content type — the base content (national and international affairs) is provided by EduForge's central editorial team and shared across all TSPs, while state-specific content (AP and Telangana affairs) and exam-specific MCQs are created by the TSP's own faculty; this hybrid model means TopRank Academy gets professionally curated national current affairs without paying for a dedicated editorial team, while still adding local value through state-level content that their students need for APPSC exams; the student sees all content under "TopRank Academy" branding with no indication of the shared/custom split; the central content is refreshed weekly (every Monday), and the TSP's editorial overlay is applied within 24 hours; if the TSP has not added state-level content for a given week, only the national content appears — no placeholder or "coming soon" message is shown
- Personal notes saved on video timestamps are stored in the student's private namespace and are never visible to the TSP admin, other students, or EduForge support; this privacy guarantee is important because students often write candid self-assessments ("I don't understand this at all — need to rewatch") that they would not want their coaching centre to see; notes are full-text searchable within the student's account, so Ravi can search "budget GDP" and find all his timestamped notes across all videos mentioning that term; notes are included in the student's data export if they request a DPDP Act data portability download; the notes storage quota is 10,000 notes per student with a maximum of 500 characters per note — sufficient for quick annotations but not for full lecture transcription
