## 5. Business Rules

- Content quality scoring uses a weighted five-component model that evaluates every question in the EduForge pool: answer accuracy (30% weight — is the marked answer definitively correct), solution clarity (20% — is the explanation step-by-step and understandable), question relevance to syllabus (20% — does the question test a topic that actually appears in the target exam's official syllabus), language quality (15% — grammar, clarity, no ambiguous phrasing), and difficulty calibration accuracy (15% — does the question's tagged difficulty level match actual student performance data); quality scores are computed using a combination of automated checks (duplicate detection, solution verification via symbolic computation for Maths/Reasoning), crowd-sourced signals (student error reports, faculty feedback), and periodic manual audits by EduForge's content team of 45 subject matter experts; a question scoring below 3.5 on any component is flagged for immediate review, and questions below 3.0 overall are automatically retired from the pool

- The error reporting workflow is designed to minimise resolution time because a wrong answer key in a live test directly impacts student confidence and TSP reputation; when a student or faculty member reports an error, the report is auto-classified using NLP into one of seven categories (wrong answer key, outdated information, ambiguous question, solution error, translation error, duplicate, formatting) and routed to the appropriate EduForge subject matter expert; wrong answer key reports are treated as P0 incidents with a target resolution time of 4 hours because they cause immediate scoring injustice — if confirmed, the correction is pushed to all TSPs via emergency sync (bypassing the daily schedule), affected student scores are recalculated automatically, and students who were marked incorrect due to the wrong key receive a push notification explaining the correction and their updated score; the 4-hour SLA for answer key corrections is a contractual commitment in EduForge's TSP partnership agreement

- The rewards program for error reports and content suggestions creates a virtuous feedback loop that improves content quality across the entire platform; TSPs and their faculty are financially incentivised to report errors (Rs 100 per confirmed wrong answer key, Rs 50 per confirmed formatting or typo issue) and submit content suggestions (Rs 500 to Rs 2,000 invoice credit for accepted suggestions that lead to new content creation); this is modelled after bug bounty programs in the software industry and is particularly effective in the Indian edtech context where faculty members at coaching centres are deeply familiar with exam patterns and can identify content gaps that EduForge's centralised content team might miss; for example, a faculty member at TopRank Academy who has taught APPSC for 15 years knows exactly which topics are trending in recent papers and can suggest content creation that directly benefits all TSPs targeting APPSC; rewards are applied as credits against the next monthly invoice, not as cash payouts, which simplifies GST compliance

- Marketplace content (TSP-to-TSP purchases) has a separate quality assurance process from EduForge pool content; before a TSP listing goes live on the marketplace, EduForge's content team performs a quality audit that includes: random sampling of 10% of questions (minimum 50 questions), checking for duplicates against the existing EduForge pool (using cosine similarity on question embeddings — threshold 0.92), verifying answer keys for the sampled questions, checking language quality, and confirming that the seller TSP owns the intellectual property (not copied from a published book or another platform); marketplace listings that pass with a quality score of 4.0 or above are approved; listings scoring 3.5 to 4.0 are returned with improvement suggestions; listings below 3.5 are rejected; the buyer TSP can also report quality issues with marketplace content, and if confirmed error rates exceed 2% (compared to 0.3% for the EduForge pool), the listing is suspended and the seller must remediate; this two-tier quality standard (higher bar for EduForge pool, acceptable bar for marketplace) reflects the reality that TSP-created content may be narrower in scope but deeper in regional exam expertise
