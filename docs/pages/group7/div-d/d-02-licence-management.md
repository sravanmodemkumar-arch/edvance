## 5. Business Rules

- Content licensing is billed on active students only, defined as students who logged in at least once during the billing cycle; this is a deliberate design choice for the Indian coaching market where student activity is highly seasonal — APPSC notifications in Andhra Pradesh cause enrolment surges in March-April, SSC exam dates cause spikes in June-July, and IBPS recruitment cycles drive Banking enrolments in September-October; a TSP like TopRank Academy may have 3,000 registered students but only 2,847 active in a given month, and they pay only for 2,847; this prevents TSPs from being penalised for dormant accounts and aligns EduForge's revenue with actual platform usage, which is critical for building trust with cost-sensitive coaching centre owners in tier-2 and tier-3 cities who operate on thin margins

- Plan upgrades are effective immediately with pro-rated billing for the remaining days in the current cycle, while downgrades take effect at the start of the next billing cycle; this asymmetry is intentional — upgrades give instant access because a TSP requesting more content likely has an immediate need (an upcoming exam, a new batch starting, a student requesting a specific exam category), whereas downgrades are deferred to give the TSP time to review and archive tests that use questions from the content being removed; when a downgrade is requested, the system generates a "downgrade impact report" listing all tests, practice sets, and scheduled mocks that reference questions outside the new tier's coverage, and the TSP admin must acknowledge this report before the downgrade is confirmed; questions from the higher tier remain visible in existing student attempt histories (read-only) but cannot be used in new tests

- Marketplace licence costs from TSP-to-TSP purchases are billed separately from the EduForge pool licence but on the same invoice for convenience; each marketplace licence has its own renewal cycle based on the purchase date; EduForge acts as the billing intermediary — the buying TSP pays EduForge, and EduForge disburses 80% to the selling TSP after deducting a 20% platform commission; disbursements to sellers happen on the 15th of each month for the previous month's earnings; this model ensures that buyers have a single billing relationship with EduForge (simplifying GST compliance — EduForge issues one consolidated invoice with 18% GST), and sellers receive predictable monthly payouts; in case of disputes (content quality issues, duplicate content), EduForge holds the disputed amount in escrow until resolution

- Usage tracking serves dual purposes — operational monitoring for the TSP and business intelligence for EduForge; TSP admins see usage analytics to understand which exam categories and subjects are most popular with their students, helping them decide whether to create more tests in underutilised areas or upgrade to a higher tier for exam categories their students are demanding; EduForge uses aggregated usage data (anonymised across TSPs) to prioritise content creation — if 200 TSPs are heavily using SSC CGL Quantitative Aptitude but barely touching SSC CGL English, the content team shifts focus to create more English questions; usage alerts can be configured by the TSP admin — for example, "notify me when IBPS PO usage exceeds 80% of licensed questions" — which acts as a natural upgrade trigger; TSPs consistently using above 90% of their pool for any exam category receive automated suggestions to upgrade, with the impact calculator showing exact cost difference and additional content unlocked
