## 5. Business Rules

- Content sync runs on a daily schedule at a TSP-configurable time (default 06:00 AM IST) chosen to coincide with the lowest student activity window; the sync process is incremental — only changed questions (new, corrected, retired, recalibrated) are transmitted, not the entire pool, which is critical given that the full pool is 18,42,000+ questions and a full sync would consume significant bandwidth and processing time; sync is designed for eventual consistency — if a sync fails (network issues, server maintenance), the system retries automatically at 30-minute intervals up to 3 times, and if all retries fail, the TSP admin is notified via email and dashboard alert; Premium plan TSPs get real-time sync (WebSocket-based push) where corrections and new questions appear within 5 minutes of publication, which matters for current affairs content where timeliness is the value proposition

- Current affairs content is the fastest-moving segment of the Indian competitive exam preparation ecosystem; EduForge's content team operates in shifts (06:00 AM to 12:00 AM IST) to ensure that any significant national or international event is converted into 3-5 exam-relevant MCQs within 4-6 hours; the CA feed is categorised into 12 categories aligned with the syllabus structure of major exams — National Affairs, International Affairs, Economy and Finance, Science and Technology, Sports, Awards and Honours, Government Schemes, State Affairs (per-state), Defence and Security, Environment, and Books and Authors; TSPs can enable or disable categories based on the exams they target — TopRank Academy in Vijayawada enables AP State Affairs and TS State Affairs (for APPSC and TSPSC students) but disables Defence and Security because they do not target CDS/CAPF exams; the auto-publish feature allows CA questions to flow directly into a daily student quiz without faculty intervention, which is popular with smaller TSPs that lack dedicated content staff

- Content corrections are the most sensitive sync operation because they can affect live tests and historical student attempt data; when EduForge's content team corrects a question (fixes a wrong answer key, updates an outdated fact, corrects a language error), the correction must propagate to all TSPs that have licensed that question; the default behaviour is auto-apply, meaning the correction silently takes effect across all tests — if a student previously answered a question that had an incorrect answer key and scored zero, their score is automatically recalculated with the corrected key and they receive a notification explaining the correction; TSPs that choose "flag for faculty review" receive corrections in a review queue and must manually approve each one, which is preferred by large TSPs with dedicated content teams (like Allen, Resonance) who want to verify corrections before applying; retired questions (removed from the pool due to being outdated, duplicated, or reported as unfixable) are handled gracefully — they remain visible in past attempt histories for audit purposes but are excluded from future test generation

- Conflict resolution addresses the tension between EduForge's centralised content management and TSP-level customisation; TSPs are allowed to customise licensed questions within specific bounds — they can add regional language hints, modify difficulty tags based on their student cohort, add TSP-specific tags, and attach supplementary explanations; however, the core question text, answer options, correct answer, and official solution remain EduForge-controlled; when EduForge updates a question that a TSP has customised, a merge conflict arises; the recommended "merge" policy applies EduForge's core corrections (answer key fix, option text correction, solution update) while preserving the TSP's additions (Telugu hints, custom tags); in rare cases where both EduForge and the TSP modified the same field (e.g., difficulty level), the conflict is flagged for manual resolution; TSPs that never customise licensed content (approximately 60% of TSPs) will never encounter conflicts and can safely leave the default policy as "EduForge always wins"
