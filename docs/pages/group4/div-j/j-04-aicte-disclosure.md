| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/college/{id}/reports/aicte/status/` | Disclosure filing status per section |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/college/{id}/reports/aicte/section/{code}/` | Section data (A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H) |

## 7. Business Rules

- AICTE Mandatory Disclosure is a public document — it is published on the AICTE portal and accessible to anyone; prospective students, parents, and peer institutions compare disclosures; data that appears inconsistent (e.g., 100% placement claimed in disclosure but low NIRF placement score) triggers scrutiny; EduForge's cross-check tool flags inconsistencies before submission
- The annual disclosure deadline (30 June) is non-negotiable; AICTE has suspended approvals for institutions that consistently fail to file; a "data in preparation" status beyond the deadline is penalised in the EoA process; EduForge automatically begins data collection for disclosure from 1 May, giving the institution 6 weeks to compile, review, and file
- Faculty data in the disclosure must match the AICTE EoA application data; AICTE inspectors cross-verify during Extension of Approval inspections; discrepancies between declared faculty count and payroll-evidenced count are treated as misrepresentation; GCEH's faculty count of 62 (vs AICTE required ~70 for 1,424 students) is within tolerance for affiliated colleges with sanctioned intake utilisation below 100%
- Admission data in Section E must reconcile with JNTU counselling allotment data; GCEH's admissions come through TSEAMCET (Telangana State Engineering Agriculture and Medical Common Entrance Test) state counselling, which allocates seats centrally; the counselling-allotted count becomes the official admission figure; any discrepancy between counselling data and institution-reported data is flagged by JNTU during affiliation renewal
- AICTE's online portal (AICTE Web) is the single submission platform; the institution must maintain its AICTE login credentials securely; the Compliance Officer holds the AICTE login; the Principal holds the Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for final certification; both must be available and functional in May–June each year for the disclosure filing exercise
