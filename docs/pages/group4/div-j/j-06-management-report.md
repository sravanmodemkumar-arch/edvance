## 7. Business Rules

- The Governing Body is the highest decision-making authority of the institution and bears ultimate responsibility for its regulatory standing; the GB cannot claim ignorance of compliance failures if it is presented with regular, accurate reports; EduForge's management report ensures the GB receives structured, complete institutional information at each quarterly meeting — the "action items" section converts information into explicit decisions
- Management reports must present both positive developments and problems with equal honesty; a report that only shows achievements creates false comfort and prevents the GB from exercising its oversight function; the fee defaulters situation (₹42L + ₹76L ePASS pending), the patent overdue, and the salary gap are included in this report because the GB needs to act on them — omitting problems is governance failure
- Financial data presented to the Governing Body must be certified by the Finance Manager and the Principal; the GB receives management accounts (not audited accounts) for in-year decisions; the audited financial statements are placed at the September meeting annually; decisions on capital expenditure, salary revision, and write-offs must be recorded as formal resolutions in the GB meeting minutes — verbal approvals are not valid for audit purposes
- The 5-year strategic plan agreed by the Trust (Top 300 NIRF, Grade A NAAC, 75% placement) must be tracked at each GB meeting; quarterly reports should explicitly state progress against the strategic targets, not just report current-year metrics; an institution that achieves its targets is building Trust confidence and attracting investment; one that drifts from its strategic plan without explanation raises governance concerns
- GB resolutions on sensitive matters (salary revision, legal action, capital expenditure above threshold) must be recorded in the minutes with member-wise voting records where applicable; these minutes are a discoverable legal document; if a former employee challenges a termination or a student challenges a fee recovery, the GB resolution authorising the action is the primary defence; EduForge's resolution tracking module maintains a searchable, timestamped audit trail of all GB decisions
