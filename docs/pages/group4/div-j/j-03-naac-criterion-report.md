## 7. Business Rules

- NAAC Criterion 3 (Research) carries 25% weight — equal to Criterion 2 (Teaching); many institutions under-invest in research because teaching is their primary function, but NAAC's weightage means that a college with excellent teaching but poor research cannot exceed Grade B++ regardless of how well it performs on other criteria; GCEH's Grade A ambition requires sustained investment in research output, not just compliance documentation
- DVV (Data Verification and Validation) is the NAAC peer team's most rigorous tool; each quantitative metric in the SSR is subjected to evidence verification — a faculty count of 62 must be supported by appointment letters and payroll records; a publication count of 50 must have verifiable DOIs; EduForge maintains primary evidence attachments for every metric used in NAAC data, accessible during DVV
- The CGPA improvement from 2.97 (Cycle 2) to 3.62 (Cycle 3 estimate) represents genuine institutional improvement across teaching quality, research output, and student outcomes; this improvement narrative — backed by data — is more powerful for institutional branding than any marketing campaign
- The IQAC must submit an Annual Quality Assurance Report (AQAR) every year between NAAC cycles; AQARs are cumulative evidence of institutional improvement; institutions that submit AQARs consistently and accurately find their SSR preparation less burdensome because AQAR data directly maps to SSR metrics; GCEH has submitted AQARs for 2022–23, 2023–24, 2024–25, and 2025–26 (pending submission)
- NAAC's "Institutional Distinctiveness" (7.4) is deliberately open-ended — it asks the institution to articulate what makes it unique; generic answers (good faculty, good infrastructure) score poorly; GCEH's distinctiveness is its regional focus on Telangana engineering education, research in IoT/agricultural technology, and consistent graduate outcomes from a Tier-2 city context; this narrative, backed by specific data points, forms a compelling distinctiveness case
