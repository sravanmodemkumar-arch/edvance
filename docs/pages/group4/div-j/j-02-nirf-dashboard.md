## 7. Business Rules

- NIRF is not merely a ranking exercise — it is the most rigorous annual data audit an institution performs; the data submitted to NIRF must be consistent with NAAC SSR data, AICTE Mandatory Disclosure, and internal records; a 3-way consistency check (NIRF vs NAAC vs AICTE) before submission prevents embarrassing discrepancies that are visible to regulators and the public
- The RP parameter (weight: 30%) is the primary differentiator between top-200 and 300–500 band institutions; institutions with similar GO and TLR scores but higher RP (more publications, citations, patents) rank significantly higher; GCEH's strategic priority for the next 3 years must be RP improvement through structured research investment
- NIRF Perception (PR) is calculated from peer surveys sent to academic leaders nationwide; a college with no visibility (no publications, no news, no alumni in academia) scores near zero regardless of its actual quality; increasing national visibility via faculty publications in reputed journals, conference presentations, and media coverage is the only way to improve PR over a 3–5 year horizon
- EduForge auto-generates the NIRF data submission package from source modules: student data from Division A, faculty data from Division H, publications from Division F, placement from Division E, financial data from Division C; this eliminates manual compilation errors and saves the IQAC Coordinator 30–40 hours per year; however, the Principal and IQAC Coordinator must review the auto-generated data before submission — auto-generation does not substitute for judgement
- Historical NIRF submissions are permanently on record at nirfindia.org; peer institutions, funding agencies, and industry recruiters use NIRF data to evaluate institutions; a single year of inflated data can damage credibility if subsequent years show a sharp drop; GCEH's consistent improvement trajectory (even modest) is more valuable than a one-off rank jump that cannot be sustained
