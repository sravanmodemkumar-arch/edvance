## 1. Internship Policy

```
INTERNSHIP FRAMEWORK — GCEH (B.Tech)

MANDATORY INTERNSHIP (AICTE Internship Policy 2021):
  Requirement: Minimum 4-week industry internship for B.Tech graduation
  Timing: Between Semesters VI and VII (Summer — June/July of Year 3)
  Credit: 2 credits (counted in Semester VII curriculum — UGC NEP integration)
  Evaluation: Report + viva (internal evaluation by faculty guide)

OPTIONAL INTERNSHIP:
  Semester internship: Some students do 6-month internship in final year
    Counted as elective project (B.Tech curriculum allows this under NEP)
    Requires HOD + Principal approval
  Summer internship (Year 1, Year 2): Encouraged but not mandatory
    No academic credit but professional experience
    EduForge tracks for NAAC outcomes tracking

AICTE INTERNSHIP PORTAL:
  AICTE has an Internship Portal (internship.aicte-india.org)
  Companies post internship opportunities
  Students apply through portal
  College must submit internship completion data to AICTE portal annually
  EduForge integration: Imports AICTE portal internship data for tracking

STIPEND:
  TDS on internship stipend: If >₹50,000/year, TDS deducted by company under
  Section 194J (professional services) at 10%; student files ITR to claim refund
  Most internships: ₹10,000–₹25,000/month (₹40K–₹1L for 4 weeks – no TDS typically)
  PSU internships: Often ₹5,000–₹8,000/month (government rate)
```

## 6. Business Rules

- The mandatory 4-week internship under AICTE Policy 2021 is a graduation requirement; JNTU (and other affiliating universities under NEP) have now integrated internship credits into the curriculum; a student who does not complete the mandatory internship cannot graduate; EduForge enforces this as a hard block on the degree-clearance checklist alongside academic requirements
- PPO (Pre-Placement Offer) from internship is a significant placement outcome; companies that offer PPOs expect those PPOs to be accepted at a reasonable rate; a college where 80% of PPOs are declined signals that students are using the internship primarily for experience, not employment — this can cause companies to discontinue the internship programme; placement cell should track PPO conversion and discuss with students about commitment
- Student internship at family-owned businesses creates academic integrity concerns — the company has no incentive to assess the student honestly and the internship experience may be superficial; AICTE's internship guidelines allow family business internships but require a third-party supervisor endorsement; EduForge flags family-relationship internships for enhanced faculty scrutiny
- AICTE internship portal data submission is mandatory; non-submission means the college's performance on internship provision is not recorded in AICTE's system, which affects rankings and compliance status; EduForge's AICTE portal sync ensures submission happens without manual effort
- TDS on stipends: If a student receives ₹30,000/month for 2 months = ₹60,000, the company may deduct TDS under Section 194J; the college must advise students to collect Form 16A (TDS certificate) from the company and file ITR to claim refund (student's income is likely below taxable threshold if no other income); missing this advice has led to students losing their TDS refunds
