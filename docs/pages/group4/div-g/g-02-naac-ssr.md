## 6. Business Rules

- AQAR submission every year is mandatory for accredited institutions; non-submission for 2 consecutive years can result in NAAC accreditation lapsing; EduForge's AQAR auto-generation from ERP data (attendance, results, placement, publications) significantly reduces the manual data collection burden
- The SSR must be evidence-based, not assertion-based; every claim must have supporting documents; NAAC peer teams sample-verify 20–30% of claims during site visit; claims with no evidence are scored as "0" even if true in fact; EduForge links each SSR data point to source documents (receipts, registers, screenshots)
- "Closing the loop" (identifying an issue, taking action, and demonstrating improvement) is the single most powerful indicator of quality culture that NAAC peer teams look for; institutions that simply collect feedback and data without acting on it score poorly in governance and quality criteria; EduForge's IQAC module tracks action-taken on every identified weakness
- IQAC must include external members (industry, alumni, students); many colleges constitute an IQAC with only internal members; this non-compliance is easily spotted during peer team interaction with committee members; genuine external participation strengthens both credibility and quality perspective
- NAAC grades are publicly available and used by students for admission decisions, employers for campus recruitment priority, and banks for education loan assessments; institutions that maintain Grade A or above consistently experience quantifiable benefits in student quality, placement company interest, and funding access
