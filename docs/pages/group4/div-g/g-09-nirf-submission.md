## 6. Business Rules

- NIRF data is publicly ranked and downloaded by thousands of students and parents; false data submitted to NIRF — when discovered (competitors and journalists do cross-check) — causes severe reputational damage and can trigger MoE inquiry; EduForge's source-of-truth approach (NIRF data automatically from ERP records, not manually entered) makes the submission consistent with actual records
- The PR (Perception) parameter is the hardest to directly influence because it depends on peer and employer surveys; however, NAAC Grade A or A+ is the single most powerful indirect driver of PR scores; institutions that achieve A+ grade typically see their PR score jump significantly in the following NIRF cycle — this is the strongest strategic argument for NAAC investment
- PhD faculty percentage is disproportionately important in NIRF's TLR2; a college can improve its overall NIRF score significantly by hiring even 2–3 PhD faculty, since this is a binary increase (each new PhD faculty directly changes the percentage); EduForge's faculty HR module tracks PhD completion progress for existing faculty as well, forecasting future TLR2 improvement
- NIRF GO parameter includes salary data; NIRF uses its own formula to normalize salary; providing accurate median salary (not inflated mean) is important because NIRF cross-checks salary bands against EPFO salary records for large employers — discrepancies trigger manual review
- Participating in NIRF is technically voluntary but practically important; non-participating institutions are ranked at the bottom by default in perception surveys (peers assume poor performance); even a Band 201–250 rank is better than no rank for institutional credibility with employers, students, and funding agencies
