## 6. Business Rules

- CO-PO attainment computation from actual marks data (not manually estimated) is the core NBA requirement for OBE; colleges that write CO-PO attainment numbers without computing from actual CIE and SEE data are fabricating accreditation evidence; NBA expert teams ask faculty how the numbers were computed and can instantly identify when they are manually guessed; EduForge's automatic CO-PO computation from the CIE marks module makes this tamper-proof and auditable
- The Washington Accord recognition is a genuine and valuable outcome of NBA accreditation; the placement cell should communicate this to students and include it in company profiles shared with international employers; many students going abroad for MS programs benefit from NBA-accredited degree recognition
- Faculty PhD percentage below the threshold (60% for Tier 2) is a common deficiency; the college's mitigation plan must include specific timelines (faculty X completing PhD by month/year) and must be verifiable; vague plans like "we are encouraging faculty to pursue PhD" are not accepted; EduForge's faculty module tracks PhD enrolment and expected completion dates
- CO-PO attainment data must show year-over-year improvement or explain why there is no improvement; NBA criterion 11 (Continuous Improvement) specifically requires that action plans based on attainment data are implemented and show results; a college that finds PO4 below threshold every year for 3 years and takes no action fails criterion 11
- NBA and NAAC are separate accreditations with overlapping but distinct requirements; NBA focuses on programme-level outcomes and engineering standards; NAAC covers the entire institution across all criteria; a college can have NBA without NAAC and vice versa; ideally both — NAAC Grade A+ qualifies for NBA Tier 1 benefits
