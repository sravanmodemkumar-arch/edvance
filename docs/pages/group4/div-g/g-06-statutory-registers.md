## 6. Business Rules

- EPF deposit delay is a criminal offence under Section 14 of the EPF Act (imprisonment up to 3 years + fine); even a single month's delay can trigger employee complaints to EPFO; the 15th-of-month deadline must be treated as non-negotiable; EduForge payroll module triggers EPF ECR generation on payroll approval (typically 3rd–5th of month), giving 10 days for review, correction, and timely deposit
- Gratuity must be paid within 30 days of the triggering event (retirement/resignation); delay beyond 30 days attracts interest at the rate fixed by the government (currently 10% p.a.); non-payment can lead to a recovery application before the Controlling Authority under Section 8 of the Gratuity Act, and courts have ordered additional compensation in some cases
- The maternity benefit records must show that no deduction was made from salary during the 26-week leave period; the Payment of Maternity Benefits is a full-salary entitlement (not a partial allowance); any deduction is an offence under the Act; if a visiting faculty replacement is hired during the leave, the replacement salary is an additional cost — not a substitute for the on-leave employee's salary
- The crèche facility requirement (>50 employees) is often overlooked by educational institutions; GCEH with 86 employees clearly triggers this requirement; non-compliance is an offence under the Maternity Benefit Amendment Act 2017; the tie-up with a nearby daycare is an acceptable alternative to an in-premises crèche — the key is that the facility must be accessible, notified to employees, and documented
- Service books must be maintained for the entire service period of a faculty member and preserved for at least 5 years after retirement; they are primary evidence in disputes over seniority, leave balance, increments, and pension entitlements; a faculty member who disputes their career history can always refer to the service book as the authoritative record — hence its accuracy is critical
