## 6. Business Rules

- The Indian Patent Act 1970 provides an 80% fee discount for educational institutions; this must be claimed at filing by declaring institution type; failing to claim the discount means paying full commercial rates unnecessarily; GCEH's patent agent is responsible for claiming this discount and EduForge's patent filing checklist includes this as a mandatory item
- Co-inventor listing must be accurate and honest; adding names of faculty who did not contribute to the invention (honorary authorship in patents is more serious than in papers) is a fraud on the Patent Office; conversely, excluding genuine contributors (student inventors, JRFs) to simplify ownership is also problematic — disputes over inventorship after grant can invalidate the patent; EduForge's invention disclosure form captures contributions from all inventors
- The 12-month deadline from provisional to complete application is a hard legal deadline under Indian Patent Act Section 9; missing this deadline means the application lapses and the provisional protection is lost; EduForge's patent module sets automated alerts at 6 months and 3 months before the deadline; the patent agent must be engaged at 9 months to ensure complete specification is ready
- IP ownership policy must be in writing and signed by all faculty at appointment; a dispute between a faculty member who claims personal ownership of a patent filed while employed at GCEH versus GCEH's institutional ownership claim is a common IP dispute; a clear, signed IP assignment clause in the employment agreement (and research policy) protects the institution
- Commercialisation revenue must be distributed per the documented revenue-sharing policy; underpaying inventors demotivates future innovation; overpaying (or excluding the college's share) is a misuse of institutional resources; the EduForge royalty tracking module records all payments and generates annual IP revenue statements for both the college's accounts and the inventors' personal tax purposes
