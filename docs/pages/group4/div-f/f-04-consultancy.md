IMPORTANT DISTINCTION:
  Tuition fee, exam fee, hostel fee → Exempt from GST (educational services)
  Consultancy fee, training to industry, testing lab → Taxable (18%)

## 6. Business Rules

- Consultancy income to the college is taxable (not exempt under educational services); the college must issue proper GST invoices, collect 18% GST from industry clients, and file GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B; a college that does not register for GST on consultancy income and collects fees informally exposes itself to a GST demand with interest and penalty; EduForge generates GST-compliant invoices automatically from consultancy project records
- Faculty share of consultancy income (60% in GCEH's policy) is professional income for the faculty member; the faculty must declare it in their ITR under "Income from profession"; the college must issue a certificate of payment to each faculty for their ITR filing; TDS deducted by the client at source (Form 26AS) belongs to the college (not the faculty directly), and the college must internally account for the faculty's proportionate share
- Confidential client projects must exclude student involvement unless the client explicitly consents; putting undergraduate students on confidential industry projects without NDA creates IP and confidentiality risks for the client; the consultancy agreement must explicitly state whether students are permitted and the NDA must cover any permitted participants
- The 1-day-per-week cap on consultancy is to protect teaching obligations; a faculty member who spends 3–4 days per week on consultancy inevitably neglects teaching, student mentoring, and examination duties; NAAC peer teams specifically ask about this during faculty interviews; the Dean Research is responsible for monitoring workload compliance
- Publication rights must be explicitly stated in sponsored research agreements; a standard clause is "right to publish with 60-day sponsor review period to allow patent filing before publication"; without this clause, the sponsor may try to suppress publication entirely, depriving the faculty of academic output that counts towards NAAC and promotions
