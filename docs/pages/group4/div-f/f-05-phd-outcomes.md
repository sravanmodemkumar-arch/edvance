## 6. Business Rules

- UGC 2022 regulations require one peer-reviewed publication before thesis submission; supervisors who allow scholars to submit thesis without this publication are violating the regulation, and the JNTU doctoral committee will reject the thesis; EduForge tracks each scholar's publication status and hard-blocks the thesis submission process until the requirement is met
- The Shodhganga submission (UGC INFLIBNET repository for PhD theses) is mandatory within 30 days of degree award; non-submission makes the degree non-compliant with UGC regulations and the scholar's profile incomplete; EduForge generates a post-award checklist that includes Shodhganga submission as a step and notifies the scholar and supervisor
- 6-monthly progress reports to the RPC are not optional; a scholar who does not submit progress reports is technically not in compliance with UGC 2022 norms and can have their registration cancelled; consistent monitoring also benefits the scholar by catching research problems early rather than after years of misdirected work
- External examiners for PhD viva must be genuinely independent; using the same examiner repeatedly, or examiners who are personal friends of the supervisor, is an academic integrity issue that NAAC peer teams investigate; JNTU maintaining the examiner panel rather than allowing college/supervisor nomination is a systemic protection against this
- PhD scholars on SERB/DST fellowships are not college employees; their JRF stipend comes from the grant, not from college payroll; they should not be listed on college payroll, should not be assigned teaching duties (they are not TA/teaching staff), and their EPF/ESI does not apply — misclassifying JRFs as employees creates incorrect payroll records and incorrect NAAC staffing data
