## 6. Business Rules

- UGC-CARE list compliance is non-negotiable for publications counting towards faculty API score for promotions; a faculty member who publishes in a non-listed journal and claims it during promotion is making a false claim; EduForge's automated journal verification (Scopus API + WoS + UGC-CARE PDF match) removes this ambiguity — the system either confirms or rejects journal eligibility before the paper is counted
- GCEH affiliation must appear in the published paper for it to count in the college's research metrics; co-authored papers where the faculty listed a previous institution's affiliation (e.g., from their PhD) do not count in GCEH's NAAC/NIRF metrics; faculty joining from other institutions should be advised to update affiliation in ongoing submitted manuscripts
- Paying Article Processing Charges (APCs) to journals that are not Scopus/WoS/UGC-CARE-indexed is a misuse of research funds and does not advance the college's research metrics; GCEH research policy should explicitly prohibit APC payments (from college funds) to non-indexed journals; faculty paying from personal funds for predatory journals still risks their academic reputation
- iThenticate (or equivalent) plagiarism screening is mandatory before any research paper is submitted on behalf of the institution; the UGC's anti-plagiarism regulations (2018) apply; NAAC peer teams ask for the plagiarism screening policy and sample reports; EduForge maintains plagiarism reports as evidence
- Inter-institutional collaborations (e.g., IIT/NIT co-authorship) count in GCEH's metrics if GCEH affiliation is listed; these collaborations should be actively encouraged; EduForge's co-author tracking helps identify such partnerships and flag them for recognition in NAAC Criterion 3 (collaborative research)
