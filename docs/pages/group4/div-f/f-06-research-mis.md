## 6. Business Rules

- The NAAC peer team cross-verifies all claims in Criterion 3 against primary evidence; claiming 50 Scopus papers but presenting only 30 with verifiable DOIs during site visit creates a major discrepancy; EduForge links every publication record to its DOI/URL, allowing the peer team to spot-verify directly; claims that cannot be verified are not counted
- Faculty with zero publications for 2+ consecutive years is a NAAC risk — it brings down the per-faculty ratio; the Dean Research must proactively engage these faculty with the research seed fund, mentoring programme, and reduced non-research administrative duties to enable them to publish; academic promotions should explicitly consider research output (API scoring)
- The research seed fund is an investment that yields returns via external grant leverage; ₹1–2L of internal funding for preliminary work often supports grant applications of ₹10L–₹30L; institutions that invest in internal seed funding typically show significantly better external grant success rates; the seed fund expenditure must be reported in NAAC as "resource mobilisation for research"
- NIRF data is permanent and publicly visible; each year's submission becomes part of the institution's national record; inflated claims, once submitted and later disproven, damage institutional credibility for years; NAAC also cross-checks NIRF submissions against AQAR data — inconsistencies are flagged as integrity issues
- The Research MIS dashboard consolidating publications, grants, patents, and PhD data in real time allows the Dean Research to intervene proactively (e.g., when a progress report deadline approaches or a grant annual report is due) rather than reactively; this converts research management from a compliance activity into a strategic function
