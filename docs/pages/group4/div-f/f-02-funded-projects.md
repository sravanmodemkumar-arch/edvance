## 6. Business Rules

- Government research grants (DST/SERB/DBT/MNRE) mandate separate bank accounts; commingling with college operating funds violates grant terms and can result in grant cancellation plus recovery of disbursed funds; the joint operation (PI + Finance + Principal) ensures both academic and financial oversight and prevents either party from misusing funds unilaterally
- Utilisation Certificates (UCs) are mandatory for releasing subsequent instalments; a college that spends grant money but delays UC submission risks delayed next instalment, which disrupts JRF salaries and project timelines; EduForge alerts the Finance Manager and PI 30 days before the UC deadline
- JRF appointment must follow SERB/DST norms exactly (GATE/NET qualification); appointing a non-qualified candidate and paying from grant funds is a violation that can trigger audit objections and recovery of amounts paid; the appointment letter must be on institution letterhead with PI and Principal signatures, and a copy filed with the funding agency
- Equipment purchased from grants becomes the institution's property (not the PI's); when a PI transfers to another institution, they cannot take grant-purchased equipment; EduForge creates an asset tag at equipment purchase and links it to the grant; the asset register shows "source: grant" for tracking purposes
- Research grants attract income tax implications for overhead amounts received by the college; overhead credited to the college's R&D fund is income (not exempt); the finance team must include grant overhead in annual income for tax purposes; individual JRF fellowships from government grants are exempt under Section 10(16) — students and JRFs should be informed of this rather than incorrectly deducting TDS
