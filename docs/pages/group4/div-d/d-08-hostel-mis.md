## 6. Business Rules

- Hostel occupancy data is a NAAC Criterion 4.1 input; understating occupancy (to hide over-crowding) or overstating (to appear more functional) both distort the AQAR; EduForge computes occupancy from actual allotment records, not from manually entered figures — this prevents manipulation
- Fire safety in hostels requires documented evacuation drills at least once per year (NBC 2016 recommends twice); the drill must be recorded with attendance; colleges where fire-related incidents occur and no drill records exist face criminal negligence proceedings against the Principal and Governing Body members
- The annual hostel report to the Governing Body is a governance requirement; NAAC expects evidence that the Governing Body actively monitors hostel welfare (not just finances); the report, minutes of GB meeting discussing it, and action taken register demonstrate this governance oversight
- Hostel MIS data (occupancy, discipline incidents, mess quality) feeds into NAAC Criterion 5 (Student Support and Progression) and Criterion 4 (Infrastructure) — both high-weight criteria; institutions that do not maintain systematic hostel records present poorly during NAAC assessment even if physical facilities are good
- The hostel capital improvement fund (surplus held for infrastructure) must be shown separately in accounts from the college's general surplus; mixing them makes it difficult to demonstrate that hostel fees are being spent on hostel facilities — a question NAAC peer teams specifically ask
