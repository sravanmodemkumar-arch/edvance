## 6. Business Rules

- FSSAI license (or registration) is mandatory for any food business with turnover >₹12L; a hostel mess serving 260+ students at ₹3,200/month generates ₹84L+/year — well above the threshold; operating without a valid FSSAI license is an offence under Section 63 of the Food Safety and Standards Act 2006, punishable with imprisonment up to 6 months and fine up to ₹5L; the license must be displayed at the food premises
- Medical fitness certificates for food handlers are mandatory under FSSAI FSS (Licensing and Registration) Regulations; a food handler who has typhoid, hepatitis A, or other communicable diseases must be taken off duty immediately and restored only after medical clearance; the college (as the premises owner) shares liability if a student falls ill due to contaminated food
- Denying meals to students with outstanding mess fees is not permissible; meals are a basic necessity for residential students; the appropriate remedy is to add mess dues to the student's account, restrict other services, and follow up through fee recovery channels — but a hostel student cannot be denied food
- Mess contractor bills must be verified item-by-item by the Mess Supervisor before payment; colleges that auto-pay contractor bills without verification create opportunities for inflated billing; the Mess Supervisor's certification is a critical internal control
- Student mess committee representation is a UGC/AICTE hostel guideline requirement; involving students in menu planning and quality review reduces complaints and improves food quality over time; the monthly committee meeting minutes must be maintained and available for NAAC peer team review
