```
  ✅ "Zero tolerance for ragging" banner (AICTE requirement)
  Verified by: Chief Warden monthly inspection

WARDEN VIGILANCE:
  First 3 months (July–September): Heightened monitoring
    Reason: Fresh students (first-years) at highest ragging risk
    Action: Warden makes 2 rounds per night (not just 1) during this period
    First-year floors: Senior students not permitted after 9 PM
    Mix-floor seating: First-year and final-year students in same block
                       (monitoring reduces if separated by block but AICTE allows mixing)

CCTV:
  Coverage: All corridors, common rooms, mess, entry/exit points
  NOT in: Individual rooms, bathrooms (privacy — DPDPA 2023, POCSO)
  Retention: 30 days (then overwritten unless flagged for complaint investigation)
  Access: Chief Warden + security team only; no access to wardens without authorisation

RAGGING INCIDENT PROTOCOL:
  Any complaint → Chief Warden immediately → Anti-Ragging Committee within 24 hours
  FIR mandatory if prima facie case (UGC Reg 9.1)
  Accused student suspended from hostel pending inquiry
  CCTV footage preserved (Chief Warden freezes recording)
```

## 6. Business Rules

- A Lady Warden for the girls' hostel who resides on campus is a UGC and AICTE mandatory requirement, not a suggestion; colleges that post a male warden for a girls' hostel or allow the Lady Warden to reside off-campus are in direct violation; NAAC peer teams verify this during site visits and mark it as a major deficiency
- Warden night round logs must be maintained as physical or digital records; the round log is primary evidence that the institution exercised duty of care; in the event of a ragging incident, fire, or student safety failure at night, the institution's first defence is the documented rounds log — if no log exists, the institution has no evidence of vigilance
- Parent notification on outpass grant and return is a best-practice safety measure; parents of hostel students have a reasonable expectation of knowing their ward's movements; the UGC has recommended this in its hostel guidelines, and courts have noted parental notification as evidence of institutional duty of care
- CCTV in hostel corridors must never extend to rooms or bathrooms; this is a clear privacy violation under DPDPA 2023 and could attract charges under IPC Section 354C (voyeurism); the college must have a documented CCTV placement policy with areas excluded from coverage
- Night supervisors (non-teaching support staff doing overnight duty) must be compensated per the Payment of Wages Act for night duty; unauthorised non-payment or delay beyond the wage day is a labour law violation; EduForge tracks night supervisor hours separately for payroll processing
