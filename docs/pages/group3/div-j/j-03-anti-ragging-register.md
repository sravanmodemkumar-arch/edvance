## 1. Purpose

Maintains the anti-ragging register, complaint records, and annual affidavit tracking. Indian regulatory basis:
- **UGC Anti-Ragging Regulations 2009** (applicable to colleges; schools adopt equivalent under CBSE affiliation guidelines)
- **NCPCR / MHRD guidelines** on preventing ragging in school hostels
- **Hostel context (H-10):** Anti-ragging register was introduced in H-10; J-03 is the school-wide (day school + hostel) register
- **CBSE Circular:** Anti-Ragging Committee must be constituted; students and parents must sign an anti-ragging affidavit at enrollment
- **Ragging definition (per UGC):** Any conduct (physical, verbal, electronic) that causes fear, embarrassment, insult, shame, torment, injury, or psychological harm to a student — especially if targeting junior/new students

Ragging in school context includes: Eve-teasing, bullying with power imbalance (senior students targeting juniors), exclusion campaigns, hazing, forced acts. Ragging and bullying overlap — both are addressed here.

## 6. Cyber Bullying / Online Ragging

```
Cyber-Ragging Protocol

Detection channels:
  ● Student complaint (J-03)
  ● Class teacher observes
  ● Hostel warden finds phone with content (H-10)
  ● Parent complaint (F-08)

Key challenges:
  Evidence collection: Screenshots must be collected immediately — WhatsApp groups
    can be deleted; victims are advised to screenshot before reporting
  School jurisdiction: Even if the incident happened on personal devices/home networks,
    if the content targets school students or disrupts school functioning, the school
    has jurisdiction under CBSE conduct regulations

Actions specific to cyber-ragging:
  1. Collect and preserve screenshots (victim provides; counsellor receives)
  2. Identify group admin (this is the primary perpetrator — created the platform)
  3. Request group be disbanded immediately (by the admin; or principal calls parent)
  4. If content is sexually explicit or targets a minor in a sexual context → POCSO (J-02)
  5. If content constitutes criminal harassment → FIR under IT Act Section 66A/67
     (school files complaint with police; does not investigate independently)

Note: School cannot legally demand students surrender devices or access personal phones
  without consent; however, the disciplinary authority extends to conduct that harms
  fellow students regardless of the medium.
```

## 8. Business Rules

- Anti-ragging committee must be constituted by the start of every academic year; the composition must include at least one parent representative, one teacher, and the Principal as chairperson
- Affidavits must be collected before a student proceeds to Class IX or is promoted to Class X and above; first-time affidavit (new student) is collected at admission; renewal is annual at start of each year
- Complainant identity is protected throughout the investigation; the accused is told the nature of the complaint but not the complainant's name until the committee meeting (where both can be present if the victim consents)
- Sexual ragging or any ragging involving sexual misconduct must be immediately escalated to POCSO protocol (J-02); the ragging register entry is made as a cross-reference only
- Principal's order following committee recommendation is final and must be implemented within 5 school days; a written order is placed in the accused student's discipline file (J-04)
- NCPCR complaint by a student/parent if the school does not act: the school must have documented evidence of its investigation and action; this register is that evidence
- Cyber-ragging incidents: the school's jurisdiction extends to conduct between its students regardless of the medium; however, criminal aspects (threats, sexual content) must be reported to police — school does not substitute for law enforcement
