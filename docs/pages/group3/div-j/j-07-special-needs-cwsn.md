## 1. Purpose

Manages support for Children with Special Needs (CWSN) — students with disabilities, learning differences, or other conditions requiring academic accommodations. Regulatory basis:
- **RTE Act 2009 Section 3:** Every child with disability has the right to free and compulsory elementary education in a neighbourhood school
- **Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 (RPwD):** Inclusive education in regular schools is the default; schools must provide reasonable accommodation
- **CBSE CWSN Policy:** Students with specified disabilities may receive scribes, extra time, exemptions from specific papers, and other accommodations in Board examinations
- **NEP 2020:** Inclusive education is a national priority; special educators required in every school
- **DPDPA:** Disability information is special-category sensitive data — more restricted than general personal data

CWSN categories (per RPwD 2016 and CBSE):
- Specific Learning Disability (SLD): Dyslexia, Dyscalculia, Dysgraphia
- Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- Intellectual Disability
- Visual Impairment
- Hearing Impairment
- Orthopedic/Physical Disability
- Multiple Disabilities
- Developmental Delay (younger students)
- Emotional/Behavioural Disorder

  - Other students
  - Other parents
  - Transport staff (except if relevant — e.g., wheelchair access need)

## 8. Business Rules

- CBSE Board exam accommodation applications have a firm deadline (30 September); missing it means the student cannot receive accommodations in the Board exam that year — the system creates an auto-reminder 60 days, 30 days, and 7 days before the deadline for all affected students
- Disability certificate must be from an authorised medical authority; CBSE specifies which professionals can certify different disability categories (e.g., SLD must be certified by a Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) registered psychologist); the school verifies the certifying authority before submitting to CBSE
- IEP is reviewed at minimum annually; for rapidly developing students (young CWSN students), a mid-year review is best practice; each review requires parent attendance and signature
- Disability information is shared with subject teachers on a need-to-know basis; the teacher is told the accommodation required, not the full diagnosis; e.g., "This student has a learning difficulty and requires 20 extra minutes — please ensure this in all tests" rather than "This student has dyslexia"
- RTE inclusion: CWSN students admitted under RTE Section 12(1)(c) have all the same rights as other CWSN students — their accommodation must be provided regardless of the school's cost
- Extra time and scribe accommodations must be applied in internal school examinations before the CBSE application — CBSE may ask for evidence that the school has been using the accommodation
- Physical accessibility: a student with orthopedic disability requiring wheelchair access must be allocated a ground-floor classroom (or a school with elevator access); this is an infrastructure accommodation that the Principal must ensure
