## 1. Purpose

Tracks all licences, permits, and No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) that a school must hold from various government agencies. Beyond the main CBSE affiliation (K-01), schools require multiple NOCs and licences to legally operate their diverse activities — transport, food service, residential facilities, events, and buildings.

The tracker ensures no permit lapses unnoticed. A school running with an expired food licence or fire NOC is operating illegally and creates significant liability.

## 7. Business Rules

- A permit that lapses is never silently ignored in EduForge; expired permits generate a P0 alert to the Compliance Officer and Principal within 24 hours of expiry; the alert escalates daily until resolved
- The application submission receipt (acknowledgement from the issuing authority) must be uploaded when a renewal is submitted; this protects the school if the permit expires before the renewed certificate is issued — the receipt proves due diligence
- School bus permits: if a school bus permit expires and is not renewed, the bus legally cannot be operated; students on that route must be told in advance and alternative arrangements made; running a bus without a valid permit is a Motor Vehicles Act violation
- FSSAI licence for a canteen or hostel mess: this is a fundamental food safety requirement; operating without it is a criminal offence under FSSAI Act 2006; the licence must be displayed prominently in the kitchen/canteen area
- State NOC/recognition: if the school's state recognition is not renewed, it cannot enrol new students; existing students can continue for one year; CBSE affiliation and state recognition are both required simultaneously — one does not substitute the other
- All NOC and permit renewals must be tracked with both the application date (school's action) and the receipt date (government's action); this two-date tracking helps identify and follow up on delayed renewals from the government side
