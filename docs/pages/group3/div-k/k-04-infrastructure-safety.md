## 1. Purpose

Tracks the physical safety of school infrastructure — buildings, electrical systems, drinking water, playground equipment, and laboratories. A school building failure (collapse, electrical fire, water contamination) can injure or kill children. Preventive inspection and maintenance is not optional.

Regulatory basis:
- **National Building Code of India (NBC 2016):** Structural safety standards for educational occupancy
- **State PWD (Public Works Department) norms:** Structural safety certificates for buildings
- **CBSE Affiliation Bye-Laws:** Building safety certificate is a mandatory affiliation document
- **NDMA School Safety Programme:** Structural audit of schools every 5 years; annual visual inspection
- **Electricity Act 2003 + BIS electrical standards:** Electrical safety inspections; earthing; switchboard safety
- **Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) IS 10500:** Drinking water quality standards

## 9. Business Rules

- Building safety certificate is annual; a school without a current certificate operates in violation of CBSE affiliation conditions and potentially in violation of state building occupancy regulations; the certificate must be from a registered structural engineer
- Any structural concern (crack, ceiling spalling, subsidence) observed by any staff member must be logged immediately as a safety issue; if it is potentially dangerous, the affected area must be cordoned off until assessed by a structural engineer
- Drinking water coliform contamination is a public health emergency — if a test comes back positive for coliform bacteria, the RO plant is shut down immediately, the school switches to sealed bottled water, parents are notified within 24 hours, and the plant is repaired and re-tested before resuming
- Lab chemical disposal: schools must not flush concentrated acids or solvents down the drain; disposal is through licensed hazardous waste collectors; the disposal certificate is retained
- Playground injuries: any injury during supervised play must be logged in J-06 sick room register and reviewed; if the equipment was the cause, it is immediately taken out of service and repaired
- NDMA School Safety Programme: participating schools must conduct a structural vulnerability assessment every 5 years; for buildings in seismic zones III–V (most of peninsular India falls in zones II–III), this assessment must include seismic resistance evaluation
