## 1. Purpose

Manages compliance with the Right to Education Act 2009 — specifically:
- **Section 12(1)(c):** 25% reservation for EWS/DG (Economically Weaker Sections / Disadvantaged Groups) in private unaided schools
- **Section 17:** Prohibition of corporal punishment
- **Section 21:** School Management Committee (SMC) — constitution and meeting records
- **Section 28:** No private tuition by school teachers
- **Infrastructure standards:** RTE Schedule (Norms and Standards)
- **Teacher qualifications:** National Teacher Education Council (NTEC) norms; TET requirement

## 9. Business Rules

- Charging any fee (including activity fees, transport fees, building maintenance fees, or any amount) from RTE-admitted students is an absolute violation of RTE Section 12(1)(c); zero-fee access includes all school facilities; EduForge hard-blocks fee generation for students flagged as RTE-admitted
- Lottery process must be transparent and DEO-witnessed; the video recording of the lottery is the legal evidence that the process was unbiased; the recording is retained for 5 years
- SMC meetings: the law requires quarterly meetings; records of meetings (minutes, attendance) must be maintained; the SMC must include >50% women members; failure to constitute the SMC is an RTE violation
- Teacher TET compliance: by MHRD's extended deadline, all elementary teachers (Classes I–VIII) must be TET-qualified; a teacher who fails TET repeatedly cannot continue in that role; the school must plan replacements
- RTE students must receive all benefits that fee-paying students receive — same classrooms, same teachers, same facilities, same textbooks, same canteen access; any differential treatment is an RTE violation and can result in affiliation action
- DEO can visit for RTE compliance check at any time without advance notice; the school must have all RTE records (lottery, admission, attendance, fee non-collection certification) ready at all times
