## 1. Purpose

Fire safety management for school premises — NOC compliance, equipment maintenance, emergency drills, and evacuation planning. A fire in a school with 400+ students and staff is a catastrophic event; preparedness is non-negotiable.

Regulatory basis:
- **State Fire Services Act (each state has its own):** Fire NOC required for educational buildings above a certain floor area or occupancy
- **National Building Code of India (NBC 2016):** Chapter 4 — Fire and Life Safety for educational buildings
- **CBSE Affiliation Bye-Laws:** Fire NOC is a mandatory document for affiliation
- **NDMA School Safety Guidelines:** National Disaster Management Authority requires schools to conduct minimum 2 emergency drills per year
- **Supreme Court orders:** Post-fire incidents in schools (multiple tragedies in Indian history), courts have mandated strict fire safety compliance

## 8. Business Rules

- Fire NOC must be renewed before expiry; a lapsed NOC immediately triggers a K-01 CBSE affiliation compliance flag and a P0 alert to the Principal; the school should not operate without a valid NOC
- Minimum 2 fire drills per year (NDMA guideline); at least 1 should be unannounced; drill records must show time taken, issues identified, and corrective actions; Principal must sign each drill report
- Expired fire extinguishers must be replaced or recharged within 3 working days of detection; an extinguisher expiry flagged during a check that is not resolved within 3 days escalates to the Principal
- Fire equipment maintenance must be done by a certified fire safety vendor; receipts from the vendor are uploaded as proof; self-maintenance is not accepted for NOC purposes
- Class teachers are responsible for bringing the class register to the assembly point during any evacuation (drill or real) — this is the roll call list; a teacher who repeatedly fails to do this during drills receives a formal reminder
- Hostel fire safety: the hostel building has a separate fire NOC from the main school building; both must be valid; K-03 tracks both; hostel drill is separate from school drill (H-08 warden duty module also covers hostel night emergency)
- CBSE inspection: the fire drill register and NOC are among the first documents inspectors ask for; they are never satisfied with "we conduct drills verbally" — written records are mandatory
