## 1. Purpose

Tracks police background verification (BGV) for all school staff who interact with children. This is a fundamental child safety obligation — the school must ensure it does not employ persons with criminal history (especially crimes against children) in positions of trust with students.

Regulatory basis:
- **POCSO Act 2012:** Schools must ensure staff with child contact have been background-verified; employing an unverified person in a child-facing role after POCSO is enacted creates direct institutional liability
- **CBSE Circular (Transport safety):** Already covered transport staff BGV in I-04; this page covers ALL school staff
- **State police verification norms:** Most state education departments require police verification for school staff before appointment; the format varies by state
- **Supreme Court (Gaurav Jain case and various):** Courts have directed schools to conduct mandatory BGV; failure to verify a staff member who later commits an offence against a child is grounds for institutional liability

Staff categories requiring BGV:
- All teaching staff (class teachers, subject teachers)
- Non-teaching staff in child-facing roles (hostel wardens, sports coaches, counsellors, librarians)
- Support staff with access to students (canteen staff, security, bus drivers, escorts)
- Administrative staff with student contact

## 8. Business Rules

- No staff member may be deployed in any student-facing role unless their BGV has been submitted to police; the system allows a conditional appointment for teaching duties (observed) while BGV is in process, but field trips, individual student access, and hostel duty are blocked until BGV clears
- An adverse BGV (criminal record, especially crimes against children) is grounds for immediate termination under the appointment letter's BGV clause; the school cannot exercise discretion here — a person with a POCSO or child abuse conviction cannot work in a school
- BGV renewal at 5-year intervals is a school policy reinforced by POCSO spirit; the transport staff (drivers, escorts) have a stricter rule (I-04) and are removed from duty if renewal is overdue
- POCSO awareness training is annual for all staff; the attendance register is a compliance document for CBSE inspection; staff who miss the annual training must complete a makeup session within 30 days
- Principal's personal responsibility: if a staff member without BGV commits an offence against a student and it is shown that the school did not conduct BGV, the Principal (and school management) can face criminal liability under POCSO for enabling the offence
- DPDPA: BGV records contain sensitive information (police verification reports, criminal record data); access is restricted to the Principal, Compliance Officer, and HR; subject teachers do not have access to their colleagues' BGV records
