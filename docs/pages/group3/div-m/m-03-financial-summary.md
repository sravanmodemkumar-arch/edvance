---

## 1. Purpose

The Financial Summary Report gives the Principal and Trust management a consolidated view of the school's financial health. It is not an accounting system (that sits in a separate institution-level accounting module) — it is a high-level management view of:
- Fee revenue (collection, outstanding, projected)
- Payroll cost (the largest expense)
- Operational expenditure
- Budget vs actuals
- Cash flow (simplified)
- Regulatory cost exposure (RTE reimbursement pending from government)

## 7. Business Rules

- The financial summary is read-only in MIS — all financial transactions are entered in the D-series (Fee) and L-series (Payroll) modules; this module only aggregates
- Payroll details are restricted to Principal and Accounts Officer — the VP sees total payroll cost but not individual salary data; this protects employee privacy in financial reports
- The trust deficit model (school revenue < total cost, gap funded by trust) is normal and expected for non-profit schools in India; the MIS report contextualises this clearly so that readers (e.g., trust management, auditors) understand the funding model
- RTE reimbursement receivables must be tracked separately from commercial fee outstanding; they are government receivables with no collection timeline certainty; the school cannot stop providing EWS/DG seats even if the government is delayed in reimbursement (RTE Act obligation)
- The budget vs actuals comparison is shown for 11 months (full year minus current month) to avoid distortion from partially-closed current month; the Principal uses this to identify variances and take corrective action before year-close
