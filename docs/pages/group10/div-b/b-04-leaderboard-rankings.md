## Overview

Multi-level leaderboard showing the student's rank at every scope: institution batch, institution overall, exam domain (All India Rank), state, and city. Rankings drive competitive motivation — the #1 reason students return daily to EduForge. The leaderboard adapts per context: JEE students see AIR across 1,84,000 JEE aspirants on EduForge; SSC CGL students see rank among 28,00,000; school students see class rank and section rank. Toppers (S6 access) get special badges, profile highlights, and mentorship access. Rankings update in real-time after each test submission.

## 5. API Endpoints

| # | Method | Endpoint | Description |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/rankings/summary` | All ranks across institutions and domains |
| 2 | `GET` | `/api/v1/leaderboard/{domain}?scope={all_india,state,city,batch}` | Leaderboard for a domain |
| 3 | `GET` | `/api/v1/leaderboard/{domain}/around-me` | Ranks around the student (±10 positions) |
| 4 | `GET` | `/api/v1/leaderboard/{domain}/distribution` | Score distribution histogram |
| 5 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/rankings/trend` | Rank trend over time (last N tests) |
| 6 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/rankings/category-wise/{domain}` | Category-wise rank (Gen/OBC/SC/ST/EWS) |
| 7 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/badges` | Earned badges and topper recognitions |
| 8 | `GET` | `/api/v1/student/badges/{id}/certificate` | Download topper certificate (PDF) |

## 6. Business Rules

- Leaderboard rankings are computed **per test** (not cumulative) — each mock test generates a fresh AIR based on all students who attempted that specific test; this matches how actual competitive exams work (JEE ranks are per-attempt, not cumulative); for students who missed a test, their last-attempted test rank is shown with a "(Mock #22)" label; the "My Rankings" summary card shows the **most recent test** rank by default, with a dropdown to select any historical test.

- Student privacy on leaderboards follows a **default-visible, opt-out model** — all students appear on the leaderboard with first name + last initial (e.g., "Ravi K.") by default; students can hide themselves from the public leaderboard via Settings → Privacy → "Hide me from leaderboard" — their rank still computes but their entry shows as "Hidden Student" to others; approximately 8% of students opt out; the student always sees their own rank regardless of visibility setting.

- Category-wise rank (General/OBC/SC/ST/EWS) is shown for government exam domains (SSC, Banking, Railways, UPSC, State PSC) because actual exam cutoffs differ by category — a General category student needs a higher score to qualify than an SC category student; the student's category is self-declared during profile setup (not verified by EduForge) and is used only for showing the appropriate cutoff comparison; this feature is not shown for JEE/NEET/EAMCET where cutoffs are less category-differentiated at the initial screening stage.

- Topper badges are awarded automatically when a student achieves rank 1 in any scope (batch, city, state, All India) for any test; badges persist on the student's profile permanently; the "Toppers of the Month" feature compiles the top 10 students across each domain for the month and features them on the domain's landing page (with opt-in consent) — this serves as social proof for new students and motivates existing students; toppers who score consistently in the top 100 AIR across 3+ consecutive tests are eligible for the "Consistent Performer" badge, which is rarer and more prestigious.

- The score distribution histogram is anonymised aggregate data — it shows how many students scored in each range but never identifies individual students; this helps students understand where they stand relative to the bell curve; for JEE, where the distribution is heavily right-skewed (most students score 80–150 out of 300), seeing the histogram helps students recalibrate — a score of 198/300 that "feels" mediocre is actually in the top 2.3%, and the histogram makes this visually clear.
