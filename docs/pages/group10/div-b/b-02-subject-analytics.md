## Overview

Deep-dive into performance at the subject → topic → sub-topic level across all institutions and domains. A student preparing for JEE who also attends school sees Physics analytics merging school exam scores with coaching mock test sections — showing a unified picture of their Physics understanding. Premium students (S5+) get full topic-level drill-down; Free students see subject-level only. This page answers the student's core question: "Which specific topics should I study today to improve the most?"

## 6. Business Rules

- Subject analytics **merge data across institutions** for the same subject — Ravi's Physics score combines school exam marks (weighted by exam difficulty and marks distribution) and coaching mock test Physics sections; the merging algorithm normalises scores to a common 0–100 scale using percentile-based normalisation because a school exam scoring 87/100 is not directly comparable to a JEE mock where 66% (198/300) is excellent; each data source's contribution to the merged subject score is transparent and shown in the breakdown ("School: 87% · Coaching: 72%").

- Topic-level drill-down is a **Premium feature** because it requires significant compute — each student's topic performance is calculated across potentially hundreds of questions tagged to 500+ topics and sub-topics; Free students see subject-level aggregates only with a "Unlock topic-wise analysis — Upgrade to Premium" CTA on the drill-down area; this is the single highest-converting Premium upsell surface (28% of all Premium upgrades originate from this page).

- The AI insight at the bottom of each subject's topic breakdown is generated using the student's performance data combined with the EduForge question difficulty model (trained on 5 crore students' attempt data); the insight identifies specific sub-topics causing score stagnation or decline and recommends targeted content (video + practice questions); the recommendation quality improves with more data — a student who has taken 20+ tests gets precise sub-topic recommendations, while a new student with 3 tests gets broader topic-level suggestions.

- Peer comparison data is **strictly anonymised** — the system shows batch average, top 10% average, and topper score as aggregate statistics, never individual students; even the "Topper (94%)" line on the chart does not identify who the topper is; this complies with DPDP Act privacy requirements and prevents bullying/pressure; the comparison data updates daily (not real-time) to prevent students from identifying peers through score change correlation.
