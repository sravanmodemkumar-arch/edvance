## Overview

AI-powered identification of the student's weakest topics across all subjects and platforms, ranked by **impact on overall score** — not just lowest percentage. A topic scored 50% that appears in 20 questions per exam has higher impact than a 40% topic that appears in 2 questions. For JEE students, topic importance is weighted by JEE exam pattern (e.g., Organic Chemistry: ~28% of Chemistry marks). For SSC students, by SSC CGL pattern. The page shows actionable recovery paths: targeted practice questions, video recommendations, and estimated score improvement.

## 5. Business Rules

- Weak topic identification uses an **impact-weighted algorithm** — not simply the lowest-scored topics; the impact score is calculated as: `(exam_weightage × question_frequency × score_gap) / time_to_fix`; a topic like Organic Chemistry with 42% score, 28% exam weightage, and moderate fix time (12 days) ranks higher than a topic at 35% that appears in only 2 questions per exam; this ensures students invest study time where it produces the maximum score improvement per hour studied.

- Recovery plans are **auto-generated by the AI engine** using the student's performance data, the EduForge content pool (18,42,000+ questions), and the video library (4,200+ hours); the plan selects content matching the student's current level — if the student's Named Reactions score is 35%, it starts with concept-building videos before progressing to practice questions; practice difficulty ramps using a spaced-repetition schedule (easy → medium → hard → mixed); the plan's estimated duration accounts for the student's average study speed (measured from past content interaction data).

- For free-tier students, the weak topics page shows the top 3 weak topics (not 5) and the recovery plan shows only the first day's content with a "Unlock full 12-day plan — Upgrade to Premium" CTA; they can still access individual practice questions and notes for free (up to the 5 tests/month limit); this creates a natural upgrade path: the student sees their weaknesses, understands what to fix, and needs Premium for the structured plan to fix it.

- Cross-domain weak topic merging works for overlapping subjects — if Suresh prepares for both SSC CGL and IBPS Clerk, and both have "Data Interpretation" as a section, the weak topic appears once (merged) with data from both domains; the recovery plan accounts for differences in exam pattern (SSC DI: graph/table-based, IBPS DI: more calculation-heavy) and includes content tagged for both patterns.

- The "Projected rank after fixing" estimate uses a regression model trained on 3 years of EduForge data across 5 crore students — given a student's current performance profile and the typical score improvement when students fix specific weak topics, the model predicts the post-improvement rank within a 90% confidence interval; the projection is shown as a range ("AIR ~2,100 ± 400") to set realistic expectations and is recalculated daily as the student progresses through recovery plans.
