## Overview

Personalised, AI-generated daily and weekly study plan that adapts to the student's performance, weak topics, exam dates, available study hours, and learning pace. The plan coordinates across all platforms — if Ravi has school from 8 AM to 3 PM and coaching from 4 PM to 8 PM, the AI plan fills the remaining hours (6 AM–8 AM morning study, 8:30 PM–10:30 PM night study) with targeted revision. For Suresh (working professional, Vijayawada), the plan fits around his job (6 PM–10 PM evening + weekends). The AI engine uses performance data from 5 crore students to calibrate recommendations — knowing which study patterns produce the best results for students with similar profiles.

## 6. Business Rules

- The AI study plan is generated using a **multi-factor optimisation model** that balances: (a) weak topic urgency (weighted by exam date proximity), (b) topic importance in exam pattern (Organic Chemistry = 28% of JEE Chemistry), (c) student's learning pace (measured from past task completion rates), (d) available study hours (configured by student), (e) diminishing returns (studying the same topic for 3+ consecutive days has reducing impact — the plan alternates); the model is retrained monthly on anonymised data from 1,40,00,000 Premium students.

- Plan adherence tracking is honest and non-punitive — if a student skips a task, the system asks "Why?" (options: "Too hard", "Already know this", "No time today", "Not in the mood") and uses this feedback to adjust future plans; "Too hard" triggers easier content for that topic; "Already know this" skips the topic in the next plan cycle and verifies via the next test; "No time today" reschedules to the next available slot; the system never shows guilt-inducing messages like "You're falling behind!" — instead, it rebalances the remaining time constructively.

- For working professionals, the plan uses a **time-constrained optimisation** — Suresh has 3.5 hours on weekday evenings and 10 hours on weekends; the AI allocates high-concentration tasks (practice tests, new topic learning) to weekends when energy is highest, and lighter tasks (revision, current affairs, short practice) to weekday evenings; the "Commute Study" feature detects when the student opens the app while in motion (GPS + accelerometer) and auto-loads 10-question quick practice sets optimised for one-handed mobile use.

- Plan regeneration happens automatically every Monday at 3 AM IST based on the previous week's performance — if a student was supposed to fix Organic Chemistry but skipped those sessions and instead took extra Maths practice, the next week's plan reallocates more time to Organic Chemistry with adjusted content (perhaps a different video or simpler practice set); manual regeneration is available via "Save & Regenerate" whenever the student changes their settings.

- The "projected rank if plan is followed" estimate uses a regression model: given the student's current performance profile, the planned study activities, and the typical improvement observed when similar students complete similar plans, the model projects the expected score and rank improvement; this is recalculated daily as tasks are completed or skipped, giving the student a living picture of where they're headed — "If you complete this week's plan, your projected AIR moves from 3,847 to ~3,200."
