## Overview

Archive of previous year question papers for all supported exams — JEE Mains/Advanced, NEET, SSC CGL/CHSL/MTS, IBPS PO/Clerk, RRB NTPC/Group D, UPSC CSE, State PSC (APPSC, TSPSC, KPSC, MPPSC), CUET, CLAT, and 40+ more. Papers are available in three modes: (1) **Download PDF** — printable question paper + answer key, (2) **Attempt Online** — simulated test in the C-02 interface with timer, (3) **Solution Analysis** — question-by-question solutions with video explanations. Previous year papers are FREE for all students — this is EduForge's top-of-funnel acquisition tool.

## 5. Business Rules

- Previous year papers are **100% free for all students** — this is a deliberate top-of-funnel acquisition strategy; students searching "SSC CGL 2025 previous year paper" or "JEE Mains 2026 question paper" find EduForge via SEO, attempt the paper for free, experience the platform, and a percentage converts to registered users (42% sign-up rate from PYP pages) and eventually Premium subscribers; the papers are the single largest organic traffic source, driving 38% of all new registrations.

- Papers are sourced from official publications within 24 hours of each exam shift — the EduForge Exam Operations team (Group 1, Division F) obtains papers from official sources (NTA website for JEE/NEET, SSC's official portal for CGL/CHSL, IBPS's website for Banking) and uploads them with verified answer keys; solutions are authored by EduForge's SME network (Group 9 content partners) and typically published within 72 hours of the exam; video solutions for high-demand papers (JEE, NEET) are available within 48 hours.

- The cutoff comparison after online attempt uses **actual published cutoff data** from the respective exam authorities — not EduForge's internal estimates; for JEE Mains, NTA publishes percentile-to-rank mapping and college-wise cutoffs via JoSAA; for SSC CGL, the commission publishes category-wise cutoffs; EduForge stores this historical cutoff data and maps the student's score to show which colleges/posts they would have qualified for; this makes previous year paper practice far more meaningful than just solving questions — students can calibrate their target score against real outcomes.

- Online attempt mode replicates the exact exam interface (C-02) including timer, section navigation rules, and marking scheme — if JEE Mains 2026 used a new NTA interface, the online attempt uses that same interface; the untimed mode removes the timer for students who want to solve at their own pace (common for first-time solvers building familiarity with question patterns); section-wise mode allows attempting one section at a time with a break in between (useful for targeted topic practice).

- Paper archive goes back to 2015 for major exams (JEE, NEET, SSC, Banking) and 2018 for state PSCs and newer exams; total archive: 2,400+ papers across 45+ exams; papers are tagged by difficulty (based on cutoff data and student performance), frequently-tested topics, and question type distribution — this metadata powers the AI study plan's "focus areas" recommendation by identifying which topics have appeared most frequently across years.
